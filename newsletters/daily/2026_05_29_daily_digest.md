<h2 style="margin: 0 0 8px; font-size: 24px; font-weight: 800; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.2;">U.S. April 2026 PCE — headline +3.8% Y/Y (highest since May 2023) and core +3.3% Y/Y (highest since Oct 2023), but M/M comes in cooler than feared at +0.4% headline / +0.2% core</h2>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #8a8a85; line-height: 1.5;">The Fed's preferred inflation gauge hits a three-year high on the year-over-year measure, but cooler month-over-month components blunt the worst-case stagflation read.</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">What is it?</strong><br>
The BEA released April 2026 PCE on May 28 at 8:30am ET. Headline PCE rose 3.8% Y/Y — the highest since May 2023 — and Core PCE rose 3.3% Y/Y, the hottest annual core reading since October 2023. Month-over-month, both prints came in below estimates (+0.4% vs +0.5% est headline; +0.2% vs +0.3% est core).
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">How does it work?</strong><br>
Personal income was essentially flat and the savings rate slipped to 2.6% as nominal PCE outran take-home pay. The Y/Y acceleration aligns with the April CPI passthrough from the Iran/Israel oil shock, but the cooler M/M signals the worst monthly impulse may have peaked.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Why does it matter?</strong><br>
This print drops the same morning as the Q1 GDP downward revision to +1.6% — a textbook stagflation tape. Annual core at multi-year highs cements zero 2026 rate-cut pricing under new Fed Chair Kevin Warsh, while the cooler M/M may cap the rates selloff. 10Y, 30Y, and DXY all reactive on the release.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Who is it for?</strong><br>
Macro and rates desks tracking the Fed-transition and stagflation trade.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">What is it?</strong><br>

</p>

Three explosions were reported east of Bandar Abbas, a strategic port on the Iranian side of the Strait of Hormuz.

<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Why does it matter?</strong><br>
Brent jumped to $96.19 (+2.02%) and WTI to $90.41 (+1.95%), erasing Wednesday's decline that had been driven by Hormuz-reopening optimism. The renewed flare-up keeps the energy-driven inflation channel live, feeding directly into this morning's hot PCE print. Read-throughs: refiners (XOM, CVX, MPC) and EM oil-importer FX.
</p>
