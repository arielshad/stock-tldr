<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #8a8a85; line-height: 1.5;">Warsh's debut FOMC keeps rates on hold but the dot plot pivots from cuts to hikes as Iran-war energy inflation pushes 2026 PCE to 3.6%.</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">What is it?</strong><br>
The Federal Open Market Committee concluded Kevin Warsh's first meeting as Fed Chair by leaving the federal funds target unchanged at 3.50–3.75% on a unanimous 12-0 vote. The hawkish surprise came from the SEP: the median 2026 year-end fed funds rate jumped to 3.8% from 3.4% in March.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">How does it work?</strong><br>
Nine of 18 participants now project at least one 25bp hike in 2026; six see two hikes. The statement dropped its prior cutting bias and added "The Committee will deliver price stability," with 2026 PCE inflation raised to 3.6% on the Iran-war energy shock.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Why does it matter?</strong><br>
The 10-year Treasury yield jumped ~6bp to ~4.49% and equities sold off sharply (S&P -1.21%, Nasdaq -1.34%). CME FedWatch now prices ~60% odds of at least one hike by year-end — a clean break from the March cut bias.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Who is it for?</strong><br>
Rates, macro and equity traders.
