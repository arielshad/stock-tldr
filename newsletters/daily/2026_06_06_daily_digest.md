<h2 style="margin: 0 0 8px; font-size: 24px; font-weight: 800; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.2;">Nasdaq -4.2%, SOX -8.7%, S&amp;P 500 snaps 9-week streak — chip stocks erase ~$1T</h2>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #8a8a85; line-height: 1.5;">The AI trade breaks on payrolls Friday — Nasdaq -4.2%, SOX -8.7%, chip names lose ~$1T as Treasury yields jump and the S&amp;P 500 snaps a nine-week win streak.</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">What is it?</strong><br>
Friday June 5 delivered the worst session of 2026 for U.S. equities — Nasdaq Composite fell 1,121 points (-4.18%) to 25,709, S&amp;P 500 dropped 200 points (-2.64%) to 7,383, and the Philadelphia Semiconductor Index (SOX) plunged 8.71%, its largest single-session drop in years.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">How does it work?</strong><br>
Nvidia fell ~6% (market cap slipped below $5T), AMD -6.3%, Intel -11.3%, and Marvell -8%; the trigger was a blowout May payrolls print (+172K vs ~80K est) pushing the 10Y yield to 4.544% — amplified by an FT report that Meta is weighing a large equity raise to fund AI capex.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Why does it matter?</strong><br>
Three things broke at once: the S&amp;P's nine-week win streak ended with a -2.5% weekly print, the "AI capex always rallies" reflex was challenged, and the rotation out of long-duration AI names accelerated. Next macro tests: Warsh's first FOMC June 16–17 and CPI June 11.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Who is it for?</strong><br>
Index allocators, semiconductor desks, and risk-parity books.
</p>

<h2 style="margin: 0 0 8px; font-size: 24px; font-weight: 800; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.2;">U.S. May 2026 Jobs Report — NFP +172K vs 80K est; unemployment holds 4.3%; prior months revised +93K higher</h2>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #8a8a85; line-height: 1.5;">May NFP +172K smashes the 80K bar and March/April get revised +93K higher — hawkish tape going into Warsh's first FOMC.</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">What is it?</strong><br>
The BLS released the May 2026 Employment Situation on Friday June 5 at 8:30 a.m. ET — nonfarm payrolls rose +172K versus ~80K Dow Jones consensus, and unemployment held at 4.3% for the eleventh consecutive month in a narrow 4.3–4.5% range.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">How does it work?</strong><br>
Gains concentrated in leisure and hospitality (+70K), local government (+55K), and health care (+35K); March and April were revised +93K higher combined; average hourly earnings rose 0.3% M/M (+3.4% Y/Y) to $37.53.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Why does it matter?</strong><br>
The beat-and-revise combo undercuts any near-term Fed cut thesis heading into new Chair Kevin Warsh's first FOMC on June 16–17 — Treasuries sold off on the print and Nasdaq futures slipped as Fed-funds futures repriced toward a prolonged hold.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Who is it for?</strong><br>
Macro, rates, and FX traders ahead of the June 16–17 FOMC.
</p>
