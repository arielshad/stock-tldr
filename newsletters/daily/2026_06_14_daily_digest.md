<h2 style="margin: 0 0 8px; font-size: 24px; font-weight: 800; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.2;">Trump Claims U.S.–Iran Peace Deal Signs Sunday in Geneva — Iran Disputes Timing; WTI -3.85% to $84.33</h2>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #8a8a85; line-height: 1.5;">Trump claims a Sunday Geneva signing; Iran's foreign ministry says no plans to travel — markets are pricing the 30-day Hormuz reopening, not the diplomatic friction.</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">What is it?</strong><br>
President Trump posted on Truth Social Saturday that the U.S.–Iran MoU "is scheduled to get signed tomorrow" with the Strait of Hormuz to be "OPEN TO ALL" immediately after. Pakistan PM Sharif confirmed final text is agreed; Iran's foreign ministry spokesman said a signing "will not happen tomorrow."
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">How does it work?</strong><br>
The reported MoU covers a 60-day ceasefire extension, a 30-day Hormuz reopening window, a 15–20-year Iranian uranium enrichment lockout, and the release of ~$24B in frozen Iranian assets. VP JD Vance and Iran's Parliament Speaker Qalibaf are named signatories in a remote/digital format ahead of the June 15–17 G7 in Évian.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Why does it matter?</strong><br>
WTI settled Friday -3.85% at $84.33; Brent -3.78% at $86.98 as Hormuz-reopening optimism overrode the war premium — the same tape that lifted the S&P 500 +0.5%. A deal collapse would re-impose the Hormuz-closure premium on crude and lift defense, energy, and tanker names again.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Who is it for?</strong><br>
Macro, oil, and FX desks with exposure to the Hormuz-reopening trade — Sunday's signing window is the binary that decides whether the crude selloff extends or reverses.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #8a8a85; line-height: 1.5;">Space Exploration Technologies prices the largest IPO ever at $135 and opens its Nasdaq debut +11%, finishing day one at $160.95 with a $2.11T market cap.</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">What is it?</strong><br>
SpaceX (NASDAQ: SPCX) priced its IPO at $135 per share for 555,555,555 Class A shares — a $75B raise that is the largest in U.S. history. Trading opened Friday June 12 at $150, hit a $176.52 intraday high, and closed up 19.22% at $160.95.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">How does it work?</strong><br>
With 13.09B shares outstanding at close, market cap finished day one at $2.11T — vaulting SPCX into the top-5 U.S. listings alongside AAPL, MSFT, NVDA, and GOOG. Disclosed financials: FY25 revenue $18.7B, adj EBITDA $6.6B, operating loss -$2.6B. SPCX also dual-listed on Nasdaq Texas, the first mega-cap on that venue.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Why does it matter?</strong><br>
A successful $75B raise with a 19% pop re-opens the mega-IPO window after a sparse 2025–early 2026 and lifts the pipeline read for filed names like Cerebras and Klarna. Nasdaq-100 index inclusion mechanics (90-day seasoning rule) are the next watch.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Who is it for?</strong><br>
IPO desks, mega-cap allocators, and space-sector investors watching the lock-up expiry, greenshoe window (~through July 11), and index-inclusion review.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #8a8a85; line-height: 1.5;">Lennar clears a lowered Q2 FY26 bar but margins keep compressing as mortgage rates and incentives bite — FY26 delivery target trimmed and LEN -4.5% on Friday.</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">What is it?</strong><br>
Lennar (NYSE: LEN), the second-largest U.S. homebuilder, reported Q2 FY26 (ended May 31) after the bell June 11. EPS of $1.24 GAAP / $1.31 ex-MTM on $7.9B revenue cleared the lowered bar, but revenue missed the ~$8.1B Street estimate.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">How does it work?</strong><br>
Deliveries rose 2% Y/Y to 20,519 while new orders fell 4% to 21,749. Average sales price slid 5% to $371K with incentives at 12.9% of ASP. Gross margin compressed to 15.6% from 17.8% Y/Y; cycle time hit a record-low 121 days. Lennar bought back 5M shares for $447M in the quarter.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Why does it matter?</strong><br>
CEO Stuart Miller cited "persistently elevated mortgage rates, constrained affordability, and cautious consumer sentiment" amplified by May's 4.2% CPI. FY26 delivery target was trimmed to 82–83K homes; Q3 gross margin guided at ~16%. Comps DHI and PHM drifted lower on the homebuilder weakness signal.

<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Who is it for?</strong><br>

<h2 style="margin: 0 0 8px; font-size: 24px; font-weight: 800; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.2;">DOJ Clears Paramount Skydance's $110B Warner Bros Discovery Acquisition Without Conditions — California AG Says Investigation Remains Active</h2>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #8a8a85; line-height: 1.5;">DOJ closes its investigation without conditions and says the deal would "increase competition" — state attorneys general remain the binding constraint.</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">What is it?</strong><br>
The DOJ Antitrust Division closed its investigation of the $110B Paramount Skydance / Warner Bros Discovery deal on June 12 with zero conditions, no divestitures, and no behavioral remedies. The Division said the transaction "would increase competition across the media and entertainment ecosystem."
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">How does it work?</strong><br>
DOJ clearance removes the federal antitrust ceiling. Paramount Skydance outbid a $83B Netflix offer for Warner. The tender-offer expiration was previously extended to July 1, 2026; PSKY rose +2.77% to $10.62 and WBD +0.45% to $26.98 on the day.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Why does it matter?</strong><br>
California AG Rob Bonta confirmed the state investigation is still active; Senator Warren called on state AGs to block the deal. The multi-state antitrust track (California + New York) telegraphed June 5 is now the binding constraint — WBD spread compression depends on that outcome, not Washington.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Who is it for?</strong><br>
M&amp;A arbs, media-sector investors, and antitrust watchers — the July 1 tender deadline and state AG track are the next events.
</p>

<h2 style="margin: 0 0 8px; font-size: 24px; font-weight: 800; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.2;">U-Mich June Preliminary Consumer Sentiment 48.9 Trounces 46.0 Est — First Rise in Four Months; 1-Year Inflation Expectations Fall to 4.6%</h2>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #8a8a85; line-height: 1.5;">Sentiment ticks up off the record floor as gasoline relief and Iran de-escalation push 1-year inflation expectations back to 4.6%.</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">What is it?</strong><br>
The U-Mich preliminary June Index of Consumer Sentiment rose to 48.9 from May's record-low 44.8 — the first monthly gain in four months and well above the 46.0 consensus. The improvement was broad across age, income, education, and political groups.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">How does it work?</strong><br>
Current Conditions rose to 48.4 from 45.8; the Expectations sub-index jumped to 49.3 from 44.1. Survey director Joanne Hsu attributed the lift to "some relief due to the early-month easing in gasoline prices." Long-run inflation expectations fell from 3.9% to 3.4%, retracing much of May's spike.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Why does it matter?</strong><br>
Lower long-run inflation expectations matter more to the Fed reaction function than a single sentiment beat — the 5-10y drop back to 3.4% is the real signal. Still, 48.9 is the second-lowest reading in series history and 13% below January 2026. The print lands four days before Warsh's first FOMC meeting June 16–17.
</p>

<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Who is it for?</strong><br>
Macro traders, FOMC watchers, and rates desks — next data points are Warsh's June 16–17 FOMC and the UMich June final on June 27.

<h2 style="margin: 0 0 8px; font-size: 24px; font-weight: 800; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.2;">Dana + Eaton Mobility Combine in $5.1B Reverse Morris Trust — $11B-Sales Powertrain Leader, $250M Synergies; DAN -12.7%, ETN +1.2%</h2>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #8a8a85; line-height: 1.5;">Dana merges with Eaton's Mobility business in a $5.1B Reverse Morris Trust — combined company targets $11B sales and $250M synergies, but DAN -12.7% on Friday as the dilution math gets digested.</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">What is it?</strong><br>
Dana (NYSE: DAN) and Eaton (NYSE: ETN) announced June 11 a Reverse Morris Trust combination valuing Eaton's Mobility business at ~$5.1B. Eaton will first separate its Mobility Group to shareholders, then Dana merges with it — Eaton holders own ≥50.1% of the combined company; Dana holders ~49.9%.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">How does it work?</strong><br>
The deal values Eaton Mobility at ~8.3x 2026E adj EBITDA pre-synergies (5.9x with synergies). Eaton receives a $1.1B cash distribution. Combined targets: ~$11B pro forma sales, ~$1.7B adj EBITDA (~15% margin), $250M synergies within 24 months. Close targeted Q1 2027 subject to Dana shareholder vote.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Why does it matter?</strong><br>
Eaton converts into a cleaner electrical/industrial pure-play (hence ETN +1.2%) while Dana's powertrain platform expands. DAN sank 12.7% on the combined-company dilution and execution risk on a $250M synergy target back-end loaded over 24 months. Dana's 2030 targets jump to $14–$15B sales and ~18% adj EBITDA margin.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">

Auto-supplier analysts, industrials event-driven desks, and RMT/spin-off arbs monitoring the Dana shareholder vote process and regulatory review.
