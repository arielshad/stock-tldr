<h2 style="margin: 0 0 8px; font-size: 24px; font-weight: 800; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.2;">CSL Limited cuts FY26 guidance and flags ~US$5B in impairments — shares crash ~18% to 11-year low</h2>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #8a8a85; line-height: 1.5;">Interim CEO Gordon Naylor uses his 90-day review to cut FY26 guidance and write down US$5B, mostly tied to the 2022 Vifor acquisition.</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">What is it?</strong><br>
CSL Limited — the Australian plasma and specialty pharma giant — scrapped its FY26 growth guidance and flagged ~US$5B of non-cash pre-tax impairments across FY26 and FY27, mostly tied to the Vifor kidney unit acquired in 2022 for ~CHF10.9B.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">How does it work?</strong><br>
New FY26 guide is revenue ~US$15.2B and NPATA ~US$3.1B at constant currency — well below prior guides of 2–3% revenue and 4–7% NPATA growth — with drags from US immunoglobulin channel inventory (-$300M), China albumin pricing (-$200M), and Middle East/HEMGENIX/iron headwinds (-$150M).
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Why does it matter?</strong><br>
Shares fell ~18% to A$97.57 — the company's biggest one-day drop on record and a fresh 11-year low. The Vifor impairment is a clear admission that the 2022 acquisition has underperformed its original thesis, and CSL's ASX 200 weight dragged the broader index lower.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Who is it for?</strong><br>
Healthcare-sector PMs, ASX index traders, and plasma supply watchers.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #8a8a85; line-height: 1.5;">KOSPI rips to a fresh record above 7,800 as Samsung and SK hynix lead an AI-memory rally that's pulled Korea's combined market cap past $4.75T.</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">What is it?</strong><br>
South Korea's benchmark KOSPI closed up 4.32% at a record 7,822.24 on Monday, breaking the 7,800 level for the first time — its fifth straight record session, with the index now within striking distance of the unprecedented 8,000-point milestone.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">How does it work?</strong><br>
Samsung Electronics surged 6.33% to ₩285,500 and SK hynix jumped 10.02% to ₩1,855,000, tracking the U.S. memory rally and reinforced by data showing Seoul's chip exports hit $8.54B in May 1–10, up nearly 150% Y/Y. Combined KOSPI + KOSDAQ market cap crossed ₩7,000 trillion (~$4.75T) for the first time.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Why does it matter?</strong><br>
Korean memory names are the cleanest global read on AI capex — Samsung and SK hynix together supply >70% of HBM. The melt-up came despite fading Iran-deal hopes, signaling the AI-memory bid is overwhelming geopolitical risk for now.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Who is it for?</strong><br>
Asia macro and global semis traders.

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">

<h2 style="margin: 0 0 8px; font-size: 24px; font-weight: 800; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.2;">China April 2026 — PPI +2.8% Y/Y (45-month high) and CPI +1.2% Y/Y both blow past estimates as Iran-war oil shock feeds through</h2>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #8a8a85; line-height: 1.5;">China's factory-gate prices jumped 2.8% Y/Y in April — a 45-month high — and consumer prices accelerated, confirming the Iran-war oil shock is bleeding into Asia's largest economy.</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">What is it?</strong><br>
China's April 2026 PPI rose 2.8% Y/Y — the fastest pace since July 2022 and well above the 1.5–1.8% consensus — while CPI rose 1.2% Y/Y, beating the 0.9% estimate; on a monthly basis CPI ticked up 0.3% versus an expected 0.1% drop.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">How does it work?</strong><br>
Production-material costs led the surge: mining prices jumped 10.8% Y/Y (vs +2.0% in March) and raw materials rose 7.1% (vs +1.1%), driven by domestic petroleum-related prices tracking Iran-war international moves. PPI also rose 1.7% month-on-month — the fastest pace since October 2021.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Why does it matter?</strong><br>
After three years of producer-price deflation that gave the PBOC room to ease, China is now importing inflation through energy — narrowing Beijing's room to cut rates or RRR ahead of the May 13–15 Trump-Xi summit and feeding the global stagflation narrative.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Who is it for?</strong><br>
Macro, EM, and rates desks.
</p>
