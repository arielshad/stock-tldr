<h2 style="margin: 0 0 8px; font-size: 24px; font-weight: 800; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.2;">Trump slaps 50% tariff on Canadian autos, alcohol and dairy under Section 338</h2>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #8a8a85; line-height: 1.5;">Trump signs three proclamations imposing 50% tariff on ~$20B of Canadian goods, effective in 30 days. Oil, potash, fish and critical minerals excluded.</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">What is it?</strong><br>
President Trump signed three proclamations levying a 50% tariff on Canadian autos, alcohol and dairy under Section 338 of the Tariff Act of 1930 — a rarely used provision for countries deemed to discriminate against U.S. commerce. The action covers roughly $20B of Canadian imports and follows the July 1 U.S. decision not to renew USMCA.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">How does it work?</strong><br>
The auto proclamation alone lists 18 pages of eligible goods; other items include milk, cream, wine and hockey equipment. Excluded are oil, potash, fish, critical minerals and goods already under Section 232 steel/auto duties. Duties take effect in 30 days, opening a negotiation window.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Why does it matter?</strong><br>
The 30-day clock sets up another round of USMCA-adjacent talks. Carve-outs shield Canada's biggest export (energy, >$130B/yr), but the auto and dairy hit lands on U.S. carmakers with cross-border supply chains and border-state consumers. The Canadian Chamber of Commerce called it "a regrettable escalation."
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Who is it for?</strong><br>
Trade desks, auto suppliers, and USMCA-exposed importers watching the August 19 deadline.
</p>
