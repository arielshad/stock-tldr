LNG names Cheniere (LNG) and Shell (SHEL) are Wall Street beneficiaries; utility-heavy indices (SXNP, DAX) already lag. Watch JKM (Japan/Korea Marker) at the Asian Monday reopen for the first post-weekend Hormuz read.

<h2 style="margin: 0 0 8px; font-size: 24px; font-weight: 800; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.2;">US July prelim UMich consumer sentiment 54.4 — 5-month high, beats 51.0 est by 3.4pts; 1-yr inflation expectations ease to 4.2% from 4.6%</h2>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #8a8a85; line-height: 1.5;">Preliminary July UMich sentiment beat by a wide margin at 54.4 — a five-month high — as gas prices eased and inflation expectations ticked down.</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">What is it?</strong><br>
The University of Michigan's preliminary July 2026 Survey of Consumers put headline sentiment at 54.4 — up from 49.5 in June, beating the 51.0 consensus by 3.4 points, and the highest reading since February. It is the second consecutive month with a ~10% M/M jump, though sentiment is still down 11.8% Y/Y.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">How does it work?</strong><br>
All five sub-indices improved: Current Economic Conditions rose to 54.9 from 47.7 and Expectations to 54.0 from 50.7, with buying conditions for durables and year-ahead business conditions each up ~20%. Survey director Joanne Hsu credited "easing price pressures at the pump."
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Why does it matter?</strong><br>
Paired with June headline CPI +3.5% Y/Y and PPI -0.3% M/M earlier in the week, this print tightens the demand-side picture and reduces pressure on the Warsh Fed to lean hawkish at the July 28–29 FOMC. One-year inflation expectations fell to 4.2% from 4.6%; the five-year held steady at 3.3%.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Who is it for?</strong><br>
Macro traders, Fed watchers, and rate-sensitive investors tracking consumer confidence ahead of the July 28–29 FOMC decision.
</p>
