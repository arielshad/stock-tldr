<h2 style="margin: 0 0 8px; font-size: 24px; font-weight: 800; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.2;">May PCE hits +4.1% Y/Y — highest since April 2023 — as core re-accelerates to +3.4%</h2>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #8a8a85; line-height: 1.5;">The Fed's preferred inflation gauge hits its highest annual headline reading in three years and core PCE re-accelerates, locking in the hawkish Warsh-Fed pivot.</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">What is it?</strong><br>
The BEA's May 2026 PCE report showed headline inflation at +0.4% M/M and +4.1% Y/Y — the highest annual reading since April 2023. Core PCE (ex food and energy) rose +0.3% M/M and +3.4% Y/Y, accelerating from April's +3.3%.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">How does it work?</strong><br>
Personal income jumped +0.7% M/M and PCE spending also rose +0.7%, with services leading goods. The saving rate edged up to 3.0% from 2.6% in April. Energy passthrough from the Iran-shock is still feeding the headline, but core also accelerated — not just a food/energy story.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Why does it matter?</strong><br>
This is the first PCE print after Chair Warsh's hawkish June 17 SEP pivot — with 9 of 18 Fed dots now pricing at least one 25bp hike in 2026. A multi-year-high headline plus re-accelerating core gives the hike camp ammunition heading into the July 29–30 FOMC.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Who is it for?</strong><br>
Macro and rates desks tracking the Fed hike path; watch the 2Y/10Y, DXY, and rate-sensitive growth names.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #8a8a85; line-height: 1.5;">Micron rewrites the memory supercycle in one print — $41.5B Q3 revenue, 84.9% gross margin, and a $50B Q4 guide that runs ~17% ahead of Wall Street.</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">What is it?</strong><br>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">

<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Why does it matter?</strong><br>

<h2 style="margin: 0 0 8px; font-size: 24px; font-weight: 800; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.2;">SpaceX (SPCX) joins the Russell 1000 after just 5 trading days — $22B–$27B of forced buying lands Monday open</h2>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #8a8a85; line-height: 1.5;">SpaceX hits Russell 1000 after just five trading days under FTSE Russell's new fast-track rule, forcing $22B–$27B of mechanical buying effective June 29.</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">What is it?</strong><br>
FTSE Russell added SpaceX (SPCX) to the Russell 1000, Russell Top 200, and Russell 3000 effective after the June 26 market close. It is the inaugural use of a new fast-entry provision letting companies above the Russell Top 500 breakpoint join after just five trading days instead of waiting for scheduled reconstitution.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">How does it work?</strong><br>
Russell 1000 ETFs (IWB, IWF, VONE) hold hundreds of billions in AUM, and third-party analysts put mechanical buying at $22B–$27B. SPCX closed at $153.00 ahead of inclusion. Russell 2000 trackers face mirror-image deletions as smaller names graduate up.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Why does it matter?</strong><br>
This compresses a typical passive-flow rebalance into a single Monday open — the first time a US-listed company of SpaceX's scale has joined the Russell indexes within days of listing. Note: SpaceX is NOT joining the S&P 500 — it lacks four consecutive quarters of GAAP profitability.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Who is it for?</strong><br>
Index traders, ETF arb desks, and SPCX short interest watchers ahead of Monday's June 29 open.
</p>

<h2 style="margin: 0 0 8px; font-size: 24px; font-weight: 800; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.2;">Apple's worst day since April 2025 — AAPL -6.15% to $275.15, ~$275B cap wiped as Mac and iPad prices jump on AI memory shortage</h2>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #8a8a85; line-height: 1.5;">Apple raises Mac, iPad, HomePod, and Vision Pro prices on June 25; AAPL -6.15% to $275.15 as the AI memory shortage finally bites consumer hardware.</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">What is it?</strong><br>
Apple pushed through store-wide price increases on Mac, iPad, HomePod, Apple TV, and Vision Pro on June 25 — the first broad consumer-hardware repricing in years. iPhone, Apple Watch, and AirPods were left untouched. CEO Tim Cook called it "a hundred-year flood" in component prices.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">How does it work?</strong><br>
MacBook Neo up $100 to $699, 13" MacBook Air up $200 to $1,299, iPad Pro 11" up $200 to $1,199, Mac Studio M3 Ultra up $1,300 to $5,299, base iPad up $100 to $449. Apple cited a global memory and storage chip shortage driven by AI data-center demand consuming available wafer capacity.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Why does it matter?</strong><br>
AAPL's -6.15% session — its worst since April 2025 — confirms consumer electronics is the loser in the AI memory wafer-allocation war. Micron's HBM-sold-out print and Samsung/SK Hynix's capacity pivot to AI memory have been telegraphing this; Apple is the first Mag-7 name to publicly pass the cost to consumers.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Who is it for?</strong><br>
Mega-cap equity traders, semis investors, and consumer hardware analysts; sympathy pressure showed up in MSFT (Xbox prices up same day) and PC OEMs.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #8a8a85; line-height: 1.5;">Singapore-flagged Ever Lovely hit by what a US official calls an Iranian IRGC drone; UN halts its 11,000-sailor Hormuz evacuation "until safety guarantees confirmed."</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">What is it?</strong><br>
The Singapore-flagged container ship Ever Lovely was struck ~7.5nm SE of Dahit, Oman on June 25 by what a US official told the Associated Press was an Iranian IRGC drone. The vessel took damage to its starboard side and bridge windows; all 21 crew were safe and no spill was reported.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">How does it work?</strong><br>
The Ever Lovely was transiting the IMO/Oman-publicized southern corridor — a route Iran has rejected, demanding all transits be cleared through Tehran's new Persian Gulf Strait Authority. After the attack, IMO Secretary-General Dominguez paused the agency's plan to evacuate 11,000 stranded sailors. Brent crude still printed -1.11% to $74.43 as overall Hormuz transit counts held near multi-week highs.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Why does it matter?</strong><br>
This is the first direct hit on a non-tanker commercial vessel since the US–Iran Bürgenstock roadmap and the first time the IMO has paused its own evacuation operation. Oil's muted reaction reflects how heavily shorts have leaned on the de-escalation narrative — a live shooting test of the "free flow" framing.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Who is it for?</strong><br>
Macro desks, tanker and shipping equities, oil traders; watch for any US response and whether transits resume on Iranian-approved vs IMO/Omani routes.
