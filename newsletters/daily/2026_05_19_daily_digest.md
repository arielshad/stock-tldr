<h2 style="margin: 0 0 8px; font-size: 24px; font-weight: 800; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.2;">NextEra to buy Dominion in all-stock ~$67B mega-merger — creates world's largest regulated utility, locks in AI data-center power</h2>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #8a8a85; line-height: 1.5;">Largest US power M&amp;A on record — NextEra buys Dominion all-stock to corner Northern Virginia's AI-power demand.</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">What is it?</strong><br>
NextEra Energy (<a href="https://finance.yahoo.com/quote/NEE" style="color: #f7ff00;">NYSE: NEE</a>) and Dominion Energy (<a href="https://finance.yahoo.com/quote/D" style="color: #f7ff00;">NYSE: D</a>) announced a definitive all-stock merger on May 18, confirming last week's Bloomberg-leaked talks. The combined company serves ~10 million customers across Florida, Virginia, North Carolina, and South Carolina with 110 GW of generation.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">How does it work?</strong><br>
Each Dominion share converts to 0.8138 NEE shares plus a pro-rata cut of a one-time $360M cash pool; pro-forma ownership is 74.5% NEE / 25.5% Dominion. Customers receive $2.25B in bill credits over two years post-close; deal needs FERC, NRC, and multi-state PUC approvals targeting close in 12–18 months.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Why does it matter?</strong><br>
This is the biggest US power deal ever and stakes out Dominion's Virginia/Carolinas footprint — which sits beneath "Data Center Alley" — for NextEra's renewables and rate-base growth machine. Management targets 9%+ adj EPS growth through 2032; read-throughs hit AEP, SO, DUK, and AEE as next consolidation targets.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Who is it for?</strong><br>
Utilities, power, and AI-infrastructure investors — and arb desks pricing the 12–18 month regulatory runway.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #8a8a85; line-height: 1.5;">JGBs lead a global long-end rout — Japan 30Y at record 4.17%, US 10Y at 4.63%, on oil + Takaichi's fiscal pivot.</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">What is it?</strong><br>
Japan's 30-year JGB yield rose to 4.17% on May 18 — a record since the tenor began trading in 1999 — and the 10-year hit ~2.80%, a 29-year high. The <a href="https://tradingeconomics.com/united-states/government-bond-yield" style="color: #f7ff00;">US 10-year Treasury</a> followed to ~4.63%, its highest since February 2025.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">How does it work?</strong><br>
PM Takaichi's request to compile a multi-trillion-yen supplementary budget for gasoline subsidies and utility relief repriced long-end inflation risk and term premium. That layered on top of Brent above $110/bbl from the Iran/Hormuz situation, sending Nikkei, TOPIX, and US Treasury futures all lower.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Why does it matter?</strong><br>
The 30Y JGB is the world's anchor for long-duration risk — life-insurer hedging, FX-hedged Treasury demand, and global duration books all have to be re-marked. The move also complicates new Fed Chair Kevin Warsh's first week with the US 10Y now within ~40bp of 5%.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Who is it for?</strong><br>
Rates desks, JPY-funding traders, and global macro funds re-marking long-duration positioning across all curves.
</p>

<h2 style="margin: 0 0 8px; font-size: 24px; font-weight: 800; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.2;">Senate confirms Kevin Warsh as next Fed Chair 54-45 — narrowest margin in Fed history; takes over from Powell on May 15</h2>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #8a8a85; line-height: 1.5;">Warsh inherits the Fed chair as the May CPI and PPI come in hot — and Powell sticks around as governor through 2028, an unusual setup.</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">What is it?</strong><br>
The U.S. Senate voted 54-45 on May 13 to confirm Kevin Warsh — the narrowest Fed chair confirmation margin in history — as the new <a href="https://www.federalreserve.gov/aboutthefed/bios/board/default.htm" style="color: #f7ff00;">Federal Reserve</a> chair, replacing Jerome Powell whose chair term expired May 15. Powell remains on the Board as a governor through January 2028.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">How does it work?</strong><br>
Warsh served as a Fed governor from 2006–2011, was on the FOMC during the 2008 financial crisis, and previously worked in M&amp;A at Morgan Stanley. He's the first Fed chair in nearly 80 years to take the role with his predecessor still seated on the Board.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Why does it matter?</strong><br>
Warsh inherits back-to-back hot inflation prints (April CPI +3.8%, April PPI +6.0% Y/Y) with Fed-funds futures pricing essentially zero 2026 cuts. Perceived as more hawkish than Powell, markets now watch how he balances Trump's push for cuts against the inflation data — the <a href="https://finance.yahoo.com/quote/%5ETNX" style="color: #f7ff00;">10Y yield</a> touched a 2026 high of 4.49% on the confirmation day.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Who is it for?</strong><br>
Rates traders, dollar/EM macro desks, and any Fed-watcher who needs to reprice the reaction function for 2026.
</p>
