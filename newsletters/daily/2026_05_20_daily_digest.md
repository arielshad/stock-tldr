<h2 style="margin: 0 0 8px; font-size: 24px; font-weight: 800; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.2;">NextEra to buy Dominion in all-stock ~$67B mega-merger — creates world's largest regulated utility, locks in AI data-center power</h2>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #8a8a85; line-height: 1.5;">Largest US power M&A on record — NextEra buys Dominion all-stock to corner Northern Virginia's AI-power demand.</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">What is it?</strong><br>
NextEra Energy (NYSE: NEE) and Dominion Energy (NYSE: D) announced a definitive all-stock merger agreement on May 18. The combined company keeps the Dominion brand, serves ~10 million customers across Florida, Virginia, North Carolina and South Carolina, and will own 110 GW of generation — the largest regulated utility on earth.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">How does it work?</strong><br>
Each Dominion share converts to 0.8138 NEE shares plus a pro-rata cut of a one-time $360M cash pool; pro-forma ownership is 74.5% NEE / 25.5% Dominion. The deal needs FERC, NRC, state PUC sign-offs in VA/NC/SC, and HSR antitrust clearance before closing in 12–18 months.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Why does it matter?</strong><br>
Dominion's Virginia footprint sits directly beneath "Data Center Alley," the densest AI-compute corridor in the US. Management targets 9%+ adj EPS growth through 2032 and 6% dividend growth through 2028 — read-throughs flow to AEP, SO, DUK, and AEE as next-leg consolidation targets.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Who is it for?</strong><br>
Utilities, power, and AI-infrastructure investors watching the electricity capacity crunch accelerate.
</p>

<h2 style="margin: 0 0 8px; font-size: 24px; font-weight: 800; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.2;">US 30-year Treasury yield tops 5.197% — highest since July 2007; S&P 500 -0.67% on inflation-from-Iran fears</h2>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #8a8a85; line-height: 1.5;">Long-end Treasuries break to a 19-year high as the Iran-war oil shock seeps into the inflation outlook.</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">What is it?</strong><br>
The 30-year US Treasury yield briefly hit 5.197% on Tuesday May 19 — its highest since July 2007. The 10-year touched 4.687%, the most since January 2025, while the S&P 500 closed -0.67% at 7,353.61 for its third straight losing session.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">How does it work?</strong><br>
Traders dumped duration on Iran-war energy spillovers and a wider fiscal deficit, with the Strait of Hormuz still effectively closed pushing oil/gas to four-year highs. The 20-year auction scheduled for today (May 20) is the next supply test for the long end.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Why does it matter?</strong><br>
Higher long yields raise mortgage, credit-card, and corporate borrowing costs while pressuring equity multiples. Citi flagged 5.5% as the next focus level; rate-sensitive groups (homebuilders, REITs, regional banks, megacap tech) carry the biggest mark-down risk on each fresh leg higher.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Who is it for?</strong><br>
Rates traders, allocators, and equity duration desks tracking the fiscal-deficit and energy-inflation feedback loop.
</p>

<h2 style="margin: 0 0 8px; font-size: 24px; font-weight: 800; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.2;">Publicis to acquire LiveRamp in $2.5B all-cash deal — $38.50/share (~30% premium); RAMP +27%</h2>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #8a8a85; line-height: 1.5;">Publicis takes LiveRamp private at $38.50/share cash — $2.5B equity, 30% premium, RAMP +27% on the open.</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">What is it?</strong><br>
Publicis Groupe announced on May 17 an all-cash merger to acquire ad-tech platform LiveRamp Holdings at $38.50/share — $2.5B equity value, $2.167B enterprise value after backing out $379M net cash. Both boards unanimously approved. RAMP gapped +27% to ~$37.59 on the May 18 open.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">How does it work?</strong><br>
The ~30% premium to the May 15 close is funded with cash on hand plus debt, with a $32.35M reciprocal break fee. Closing targeted by year-end 2026 subject to shareholder vote and U.S./non-U.S. antitrust + CFIUS clearance. Publicis says the deal is accretive to headline EPS from year one.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Why does it matter?</strong><br>
Largest ad-tech deal in years — Publicis is folding RAMP's RampID identity graph into its CoreAI "agentic transformation" stack. With ~2.4% spread remaining to the $38.50 deal price, merger-arb desks are active. Comp names TTD, MGNI, and PUBM screen as next-leg consolidation targets.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Who is it for?</strong><br>
Merger-arb desks and ad-tech holders watching the data/identity consolidation cycle accelerate.
</p>
