<h2 style="margin: 0 0 8px; font-size: 24px; font-weight: 800; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.2;">Strait of Hormuz tanker traffic surges to 20 ships Thursday — highest since June 2 — as 3 Saudi VLCCs carry 6M barrels; VP Vance says ~12.5M barrels have flowed since June 17 MOU</h2>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #8a8a85; line-height: 1.5;">Real shipping data confirms the Iran MOU is moving oil — but only at a fraction of pre-war Hormuz flow.</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">What is it?</strong><br>
Kpler vessel-tracking data shows the Strait of Hormuz saw 20 tanker transits on Thursday June 18 — the highest single-day total since June 2 — as three Saudi-flagged VLCCs operated by Bahri carried ~6 million barrels through the lane. VP Vance confirmed ~12.5M barrels have flowed since the MOU signing.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">How does it work?</strong><br>
On Friday June 19, five Iranian supertankers re-enabled their AIS transponders and were observed departing the region for the first time in months. QatarEnergy's LNG carrier Mraikh and two COSCO-managed product tankers also threaded through the designated crossing route.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Why does it matter?</strong><br>
Pre-war Hormuz averaged ~120 transits/day; Thursday's 20 is still a fraction of normal. BIMCO warns the central deep-water channel remains mined (~80 mines, 40–50 days to clear) and 500+ commercial vessels with ~11,000 seafarers remain stuck in the Gulf.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Who is it for?</strong><br>
Energy desks, tanker and shipping equity holders (FRO, STNG, INSW), and commodity macro traders watching WTI and Brent throughput pricing.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">What is it?</strong><br>
U.S. equities rallied broadly on Thursday June 18 into the Juneteenth holiday close. S&P 500 +1.08% to 7,500.58, Nasdaq Composite +1.91% to 26,517.93, Dow +0.14% to 51,564.70, and the small-cap Russell 2000 led with +2.12% to 2,979.77 — reversing most of Wednesday's hawkish-FOMC selloff.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">How does it work?</strong><br>
Two catalysts drove the bounce: Intel surged +10.64% on Trump's Apple-Intel chip claim re-igniting the AI trade, and Trump signed the U.S.–Iran interim MOU at Versailles, formally reopening the Strait of Hormuz. Brent fell toward $79 and WTI held above $75, taking U.S. gasoline back below $4/gal.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Why does it matter?</strong><br>
The Russell 2000's 2.12% outperformance is the clearest signal that small caps are catching a bid on the Iran-deal disinflation impulse. Thursday's close is the last U.S. equity print before the post-Juneteenth Monday open — and before next week's May PCE report on June 27.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Who is it for?</strong><br>
Equity, small-cap and rates traders; next major catalyst is the May PCE inflation print on Thursday June 27, with Micron earnings Wednesday June 25 as the mid-week AI pulse check.
</p>

<h2 style="margin: 0 0 8px; font-size: 24px; font-weight: 800; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.2;">Bank of England holds at 3.75% — 7-2 vote with Pill and Greene dissenting for a 25bp hike; GBP/USD -0.58% to 1.3213 (3-month low), FTSE 100 -1%+</h2>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #8a8a85; line-height: 1.5;">A 7-2 hold with two voices already pulling for a hike — the BoE has joined Warsh's hawkish swing on the same day.</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">What is it?</strong><br>
The Bank of England's MPC voted 7-2 to keep Bank Rate at 3.75% on June 18. Chief Economist Huw Pill and external member Megan Greene dissented in favor of a 25bp hike to 4.00%, with UK CPI running at 2.8% Y/Y — well above the 2% target.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">How does it work?</strong><br>
The MPC minutes flagged that energy effects from the Iran war are still feeding through UK inflation, while the seven-vote majority opted to wait. The decision came less than 24 hours after the Fed's own hawkish SEP revision on Warsh's first FOMC.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Why does it matter?</strong><br>
GBP/USD dropped 0.58% to 1.3213, its weakest since March 13, and the FTSE 100 fell more than 100 points intraday. The emerging Fed-BoE policy divergence — both central banks now containing hawkish dissents instead of dovish ones — is a hard regime change for GBP carry trades and UK gilts.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Who is it for?</strong><br>
Macro traders, GBP/cable books, and gilts desks tracking UK rate policy and sterling direction into the next BoE meeting.
</p>
