<h2 style="margin: 0 0 8px; font-size: 24px; font-weight: 800; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.2;">U.S. May 2026 PCE — headline +4.1% Y/Y (highest since April 2023) and core +3.4% Y/Y (acceleration from +3.3% prior) on +0.4% / +0.3% M/M; personal income jumps +0.7% as Iran-shock energy still feeds into the Fed's preferred inflation gauge</h2>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #8a8a85; line-height: 1.5;">The Fed's preferred inflation gauge hits its highest annual headline reading in three years and core PCE re-accelerates, locking in the hawkish Warsh-Fed pivot.</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">What is it?</strong><br>
The BEA released May 2026 PCE on June 25, with headline at +4.1% Y/Y — the highest annual reading since April 2023 — and core PCE at +3.4% Y/Y, accelerating from April's +3.3% and the hottest annual core since September 2023.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">How does it work?</strong><br>
Core PCE M/M was +0.3% while personal income jumped +0.7% ($181.6B) and PCE spending rose +0.7%; the saving rate edged up to 3.0% as income kept pace with spending even as Iran-war energy passthrough remained in the data.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Why does it matter?</strong><br>
This is the first PCE print after Chair Warsh's hawkish June 17 SEP pivot, and a multi-year-high headline plus re-accelerating core gives the hike camp ammunition for the July FOMC — reinforcing BofA's call for three 25bp hikes (Sept/Oct/Dec) to 4.25–4.50%.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Who is it for?</strong><br>
Macro and rates desks tracking the Fed hike path and stagflation tape.
</p>
