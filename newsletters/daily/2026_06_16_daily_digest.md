<h2 style="margin: 0 0 8px; font-size: 24px; font-weight: 800; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.2;">Trump confirms U.S.–Iran deal 'now complete' — naval blockade ends immediately, Strait of Hormuz reopens toll-free; WTI -4.8% to $80.80, S&P 500 futures +0.97%</h2>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #8a8a85; line-height: 1.5;">Trump declares the U.S.–Iran deal complete, orders the naval blockade lifted immediately and the Strait of Hormuz reopened; Iran confirms.</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">What is it?</strong><br>
Late Sunday June 14, President Trump posted on Truth Social that 'The Deal with the Islamic Republic of Iran is now complete,' authorizing the immediate removal of the U.S. naval blockade and toll-free reopening of the Strait of Hormuz. Iran's deputy FM Kazem Gharibabadi confirmed the MoU, saying military operations end 'immediately and permanently' beginning Sunday night.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">How does it work?</strong><br>
Per the MoU: the blockade lifts in parallel with Hormuz reopening; Washington releases ~$25B in frozen Iranian assets and waives oil-export sanctions; Tehran halts new uranium enrichment and dilutes its 60%-enriched stockpile in a 60-day window toward a final comprehensive deal. Formal signing is set for June 19 in Switzerland.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Why does it matter?</strong><br>
WTI fell 4.8% to $80.80/bbl and Brent dropped 3.9% to $83.89 — the lowest since early March — while VIX fell 9.05% to 17.68 and Russell 2000 futures hit a record high. Reopening Hormuz (which carries ~20% of global oil traffic) removes the structural bid under energy prices and the haven premium under the dollar.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Who is it for?</strong><br>
Macro, oil, FX, and equity-index desks pricing the end of the four-month Hormuz closure and its downstream effects on rates, inflation, and risk appetite.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #8a8a85; line-height: 1.5;">Dow notches a record close as Wall Street trades through the U.S.–Iran deal — Nasdaq logs best session since March 31.</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #8a8a85; line-height: 1.5;">Fox bids $22B EV for Roku at $160/share to staple Tubi onto the largest U.S. streaming OS — FOXA dumps 18% on dilution and balance-sheet stretch.</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">What is it?</strong><br>
Fox Corporation announced on June 15 a definitive agreement to acquire Roku for $22B in enterprise value, paying $160 per ROKU share ($96 in cash + 0.9693 FOXA Class A shares). Both boards unanimously approved, with a target H1 2027 close pending shareholder and regulatory review.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">How does it work?</strong><br>
The $160 offer is a 28% premium to ROKU's June 10 close; existing FOXA holders own 73% of the combined company post-close, ROKU holders 27%. Fox expects ~$400M in run-rate cost synergies from stapling Tubi, FOX News, and live sports (NFL/MLB/FIFA) onto Roku's 100M+ household CTV OS — at pro-forma net leverage of 2.8× at close.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Why does it matter?</strong><br>
The deal makes the combined entity the #3 U.S. TV company by viewing share — the most consequential CTV consolidation since Disney bought Hulu out. FOXA's 18.4% dump reflects dilution and integration risk; ROKU traded ~12% below the offer price, implying ~88% deal-completion probability.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Who is it for?</strong><br>
Media and CTV desks, M&amp;A arbs trading the ROKU spread, and FOXA shareholders evaluating dilution and leverage risk.
</p>

<h2 style="margin: 0 0 8px; font-size: 24px; font-weight: 800; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.2;">Lennar Q2 FY26 — EPS $1.24 / $7.9B revenue clear lowered bar but gross margin slips to 15.6%; FY26 deliveries trimmed to 82–83K; LEN -4.5% to $93.12</h2>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #8a8a85; line-height: 1.5;">Lennar clears a lowered Q2 FY26 bar but margins keep compressing as mortgage rates and incentives bite — FY26 delivery target trimmed and LEN -4.5% on Friday.</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">What is it?</strong><br>
Lennar (NYSE: LEN), the second-largest U.S. homebuilder, reported Q2 FY26 (quarter ended May 31) with $1.24 EPS and $7.9B in revenue — inside guidance but below Street estimates near $8.1B. Gross margin on home sales compressed to 15.6% from 17.8% Y/Y as land costs rose and average sales price fell 5% Y/Y to $371K.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">How does it work?</strong><br>
Deliveries rose 2% Y/Y to 20,519 homes (in guide) while new orders fell 4% to 21,749 and incentives hit 12.9% of ASP. FY26 delivery target was trimmed to 82,000–83,000 homes; Lennar bought back 5M shares for $447M and ended with $1.8B cash and a record-low 121-day cycle time.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Why does it matter?</strong><br>
CEO Stuart Miller flagged 'persistently elevated mortgage rates, constrained affordability, and cautious consumer sentiment' compounded by a 4.2% May CPI print — the margin compression and trimmed FY guide sent LEN -4.52% to $93.12 and dragged comps DHI and PHM lower as a homebuilder-sector signal.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Who is it for?</strong><br>
Homebuilder analysts, rate-sensitive equity desks, and REIT/housing-data traders watching for Q3 gross-margin recovery at ~16% and the FOMC June 16–17 impact on mortgage-rate expectations.
</p>
