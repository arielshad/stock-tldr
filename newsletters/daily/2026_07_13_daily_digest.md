<h2 style="margin: 0 0 8px; font-size: 24px; font-weight: 800; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.2;">Iran retaliates across 6 Gulf states after US bombs ~140 Iranian targets — WTI +3.2% to $73.70, Brent +3.2% to $78.45</h2>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #8a8a85; line-height: 1.5;">Broadest single-night exchange of the war: US bombs ~140 Iranian sites, Iran hits 6 GCC states, oil gaps up 3.2% Sunday.</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">What is it?</strong><br>
The US and Iran traded their heaviest strikes since the June ceasefire on Saturday night into Sunday July 12. CENTCOM said American forces hit roughly 140 Iranian military targets — missile and drone launch sites, ammunition dumps, IRGC fast boats around the Strait of Hormuz, and sites on Qeshm Island and near Bandar Abbas.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">How does it work?</strong><br>
Iran's IRGC replied with ballistic missiles and armed drones targeting Al Udeid Air Base in Qatar, Prince Hassan Air Base in Jordan, a Kuwait Oil Company offshore platform near Shuwaikh Port (1 worker injured), and facilities in Bahrain, Oman, and the UAE. Qatar reported 3 civilians hurt by shrapnel from Patriot interceptions.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Why does it matter?</strong><br>
WTI Sep futures jumped 3.2% to $73.70 and Brent Sep +3.2% to $78.45; Dow futures fell 100 pts (-0.19%), S&P 500 -0.27%, Nasdaq -0.48%. Rapidan Energy flagged "a lot of complacency" with only 22 tankers transiting Hormuz vs a ~33/day norm. All six GCC governments condemned the attacks, raising the cost of further escalation heading into Tuesday's US June CPI and Q2 big-bank earnings.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Who is it for?</strong><br>
Macro, oil, defense, and geopolitical-risk desks tracking Hormuz traffic, energy futures, and the June ceasefire unraveling in real time.

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #8a8a85; line-height: 1.5;">SK Hynix lists on Nasdaq with a $26.5B raise — a new record for foreign IPOs and a fresh AI-memory anchor for US investors.</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">What is it?</strong><br>
SK Hynix, the world's #2 memory maker and Nvidia's primary high-bandwidth-memory (HBM) supplier, priced its US IPO of 177.9M ADSs (each = 1/10 of a Seoul common share) at $149, raising ~$26.5B — eclipsing Alibaba's $25B 2014 record as the largest ever US IPO by a foreign company. Regular-way trading begins today as SKHY.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">How does it work?</strong><br>
Books were ~7x oversubscribed and Bloomberg reports it was the first US IPO priced at a premium to the home-market reference. The offering was structured to give US institutions direct exposure to the HBM/DRAM cycle without Korean withholding tax or index rebalancing friction.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Why does it matter?</strong><br>
QQQ/NDX/index mandates face a ~15-day fast-track inclusion window, forcing buying across memory-adjacent names — MU +4.5%, SNDK +7.6%, SMH +2.5% on the IPO day. The $26.5B print raises the ceiling for future Korean and Taiwanese mega-cap US secondaries.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Who is it for?</strong><br>
Semis desks, IPO and allocations teams, and Nvidia supply-chain analysts tracking the HBM/DRAM demand cycle directly in a US-listed wrapper.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #8a8a85; line-height: 1.5;">Leaked Meta memo says its AI build-out costs half of what Wall Street penciled in — and the custom 'Iris' chip tapes out in September.</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">

<h2 style="margin: 0 0 8px; font-size: 24px; font-weight: 800; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.2;">Circle (CRCL) +4.97% to $66.14 — OCC grants final approval for Circle National Trust; first national trust charter for a US stablecoin issuer, joining Anchorage Digital</h2>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #8a8a85; line-height: 1.5;">Circle wins the first OCC national trust charter for a US stablecoin issuer, unlocking federally supervised USDC custody.</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">What is it?</strong><br>
The OCC granted Circle final approval on July 10 to establish First National Digital Currency Bank, N.A. (dba Circle National Trust), converting Circle from a state-regulated stablecoin issuer into a directly federally supervised national trust bank — the first such charter for a US stablecoin operator.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">How does it work?</strong><br>
Circle filed the application June 30, 2025 and received conditional approval in December 2025. The trust will initially provide fiduciary digital asset custody for Circle and affiliates, then expand to institutional clients such as banks. USDC has $73.2B in circulation — the second-largest stablecoin.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Why does it matter?</strong><br>
The charter places Circle's stablecoin operations under primary OCC supervision, materially reducing counterparty dependence on third-party custody banks and clearing a path for OCC oversight of USDC reserves. CRCL hit an intraday high of $72.85 (+15.6%) before fading to a +4.97% close at $66.14.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Who is it for?</strong><br>
Stablecoin desks, crypto ETF flows watchers, and financial-regulation trackers monitoring the US digital-asset custody landscape.
</p>
