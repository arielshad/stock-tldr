<h2 style="margin: 0 0 8px; font-size: 24px; font-weight: 800; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.2;">US-Iran Ceasefire Frays: Kiku Tanker Hit in Hormuz, IRGC Fires on Gulf Bases</h2>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #8a8a85; line-height: 1.5;">The June 22 Bürgenstock 60-day MOU is cracking — second tanker strike in three days triggered a second round of US-Iran tit-for-tat exchanges, with IRGC missiles landing on bases in Kuwait and Bahrain.</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">What is it?</strong><br>
An Iranian drone struck the Greek-owned, Panama-flagged VLCC Kiku (300,866 DWT, ~2M barrels of Qatari crude) in the Strait of Hormuz Saturday June 27 at 4:30 a.m. ET. CENTCOM responded with airstrikes on 10 Iranian military sites; early Sunday, the IRGC fired missiles and drones at US bases in Kuwait and Bahrain.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">How does it work?</strong><br>
Bridge damage to the Kiku, no casualties; Kuwait intercepted two ballistic missiles and Bahrain's Foreign Ministry confirmed drones at dawn. The Joint Maritime Information Center raised its Hormuz threat level back to Substantial from Moderate.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Why does it matter?</strong><br>
Brent settled at $71.99 and WTI at $69.23 Friday on the assumption Hormuz traffic was normalizing — this puts a war-risk premium back into Sunday's futures open. Any move to halt the 60-day Bürgenstock talks would force markets to re-price the entire post-MOU oil-down trade.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Who is it for?</strong><br>
Oil desks, macro traders, and defense names watching whether the ceasefire framework survives the weekend.
</p>

<h2 style="margin: 0 0 8px; font-size: 24px; font-weight: 800; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.2;">SpaceX (SPCX) Joins Russell 1000 Today — $22B–$27B of Forced Buying at the Open</h2>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #8a8a85; line-height: 1.5;">SpaceX hits the Russell 1000 after just five trading days under FTSE Russell's new fast-track rule, forcing index trackers to rebalance for the Monday June 29 open — today.</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">What is it?</strong><br>
FTSE Russell added SpaceX (SPCX) to the Russell 1000, Russell Top 200, and Russell 3000 effective after the close Friday June 26. It is the inaugural use of a new fast-entry provision that allows companies above the Russell Top 500 breakpoint to join after just five trading days of listing.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">How does it work?</strong><br>
With Russell 1000 ETFs (IWB, IWF, VONE) holding hundreds of billions in AUM, third-party analysts put the mechanical buying requirement at $22B–$27B, all compressing into Monday's open. SPCX closed at $153.00 ahead of the rebalance.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Why does it matter?</strong><br>
This is the first time a US-listed company of SpaceX's scale has joined the Russell indexes within days of its IPO, compressing the usual passive-flow rebalance into a single session. Note: SpaceX is not joining the S&amp;P 500 — it lacks four straight quarters of GAAP profitability, the committee's gating test.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Who is it for?</strong><br>
Index traders, ETF arb desks, and SPCX short interest watchers who need to size the Monday flow.

<h2 style="margin: 0 0 8px; font-size: 24px; font-weight: 800; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.2;">SCOTUS 7-2 Guts Roundup Lawsuits — Bayer (BAYRY) +17%, Biggest Day Since 2003</h2>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #8a8a85; line-height: 1.5;">SCOTUS 7-2 in Monsanto v. Durnell: FIFRA federal preemption kills the failure-to-warn theory behind tens of thousands of Bayer cancer lawsuits. BAYRY surges ~17% in Frankfurt, trading halted for volatility.</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">What is it?</strong><br>
The Supreme Court ruled 7-2 in Monsanto Co. v. Durnell that the Federal Insecticide, Fungicide, and Rodenticide Act (FIFRA) expressly preempts state-law failure-to-warn claims when EPA has determined a pesticide label does not need a particular warning. The Court vacated a $1.25M Missouri verdict against Bayer.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">How does it work?</strong><br>
Justice Kavanaugh wrote for the majority (Roberts, Thomas, Alito, Sotomayor, Kagan, Barrett joining), holding that state warnings "in addition to or different from" EPA-mandated labels are preempted. Bayer said the ruling "should result in the dismissal of current warning-based claims" and works alongside its $7.25B February 2026 class-settlement proposal.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Why does it matter?</strong><br>
Bayer faces 100,000+ pending Roundup claims and has paid &gt;$10B in verdicts since 2018 — failure-to-warn was the engine for most of them. Read-throughs extend to Syngenta paraquat litigation, 3M PFAS labeling claims, and any FIFRA-regulated agro-chemical.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Who is it for?</strong><br>
European pharma desks, M&amp;A-arb watching Bayer split-up chatter, agro-chemical PMs, and regulatory-policy traders.
</p>
