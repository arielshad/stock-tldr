<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #8a8a85; line-height: 1.5;">Japan's Nikkei smashes through 65,000 in holiday-thinned Asia trading as Trump's Sunday Iran note crushes oil and stokes risk-on.</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">What is it?</strong><br>
Japan's Nikkei 225 closed above 65,000 for the first time in its history on Monday May 25, jumping 2.87% to 65,158.19 with an intraday peak near 65,254. U.S. markets were closed for Memorial Day, leaving Asia and Continental Europe to drive the global tape.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">How does it work?</strong><br>
Trump's Sunday Truth Social post called U.S.–Iran talks "orderly and constructive," cratering WTI -5.77% to ~$91.00 and Brent below $100. Semis led the Tokyo bid — Kioxia +5.94% — while Taiwan's TAIEX jumped +2.91% to a record and Europe's DAX opened +1.6%.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Why does it matter?</strong><br>
First-ever close above 65,000 caps an ~5% surge in three weeks, with the index now ~24% above its February 28 pre-war low. The trigger pair — softer Iran tone plus falling oil — directly reverses the energy-inflation bid that has kept U.S. 30Y yields above 5%; JPMorgan now targets Nikkei 70,000 by year-end.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Who is it for?</strong><br>
Asia-Pacific equity traders, global macro desks, and energy-linked equity investors watching the oil/risk correlation.

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #8a8a85; line-height: 1.5;">Trump declares U.S.–Iran deal 'largely negotiated' with Strait of Hormuz reopening; Iran frames it as a 30–60 day framework, nuclear still off the table.</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">What is it?</strong><br>
President Trump posted Saturday May 23 that a U.S.–Iran agreement is "largely negotiated" and that "the Strait of Hormuz will be opened," capping a multi-week diplomatic push that has rattled global energy markets and pushed U.S. headline CPI to +3.8% Y/Y.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">How does it work?</strong><br>
Iran's foreign ministry called it a 30–60 day framework — not a final deal — with nuclear issues explicitly off the table and sanctions relief a fixed Iranian demand. Iran's Fars news agency disputed the Hormuz framing, saying the strait "will still be under Iranian management."
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Why does it matter?</strong><br>
Brent settled Friday at $103.54 (-5%+ on the week) and WTI at $96.60 (-8%+ on the week); a confirmed deal would pressure Brent toward the $90s and unwind the inflation pass-through from April PPI of +6.0% Y/Y. The framing gap between Trump and Tehran on uranium enrichment and Hormuz tolls is what desks are pricing this week.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Who is it for?</strong><br>
Macro / energy / FX traders, oil and equity-index desks, crypto (BTC +1.79% Saturday on the news).

<h2 style="margin: 0 0 8px; font-size: 24px; font-weight: 800; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.2;">Kevin Warsh sworn in as Fed Chair at the White House — first WH ceremony since Greenspan 1987</h2>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #8a8a85; line-height: 1.5;">Warsh takes the Fed gavel at a White House ceremony — symbolism that has Powell-era veterans watching independence cues.</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">What is it?</strong><br>
President Trump hosted Kevin Warsh's swearing-in in the White House East Room on May 22, with Supreme Court Justice Clarence Thomas administering the oath — the first time a Fed Chair was inaugurated at the White House since Reagan's 1987 ceremony for Alan Greenspan.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">How does it work?</strong><br>
Trump said "I want Kevin to be totally independent" at the ceremony, then later stumped for faster rate cuts at a rally; Warsh confirmed his intent for "price stability and maximum employment … with wisdom and clarity, independence and resolve." He inherits a curve where the 30Y has been making 19-year highs and headline CPI is +3.8% Y/Y.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Why does it matter?</strong><br>
The optics of the East Room ceremony followed by the President publicly directing the rate path are exactly the tension the bond market is pricing. The April FOMC minutes showed an 8–4 hold with the most dissents since October 1992; the June 16–17 FOMC dot plot will be Warsh's first real signal.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Who is it for?</strong><br>
Macro traders, rates desks, and Fed-independence watchers tracking the political economy of monetary policy.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #8a8a85; line-height: 1.5;">SEC blinks on tokenized stocks — exchanges flag shareholder-rights gaps and the agency holds the Innovation Exemption past its planned May release.</p>

<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">What is it?</strong><br>
Bloomberg reported May 22 that the SEC paused its Innovation Exemption framework for tokenized stocks after stock-exchange officials and market participants raised concerns; SEC staff had been on track to release the framework as soon as this week, sending BTC down ~3.6% and COIN ~4% on Friday.

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">

The most contentious provision would have allowed third-party tokens — tokenized shares issued without the company's involvement — raising exchange-side concerns about how dividends, voting rights, and shareholder protections would actually be administered on blockchain venues. The framework is delayed, not withdrawn.
</p>
