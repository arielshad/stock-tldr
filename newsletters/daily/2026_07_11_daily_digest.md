<h2 style="margin: 0 0 8px; font-size: 24px; font-weight: 800; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.2;">SK Hynix (SKHY / SKHYV) Nasdaq debut — ADSs priced at $149 raise ~$26.5B, the largest ever US IPO by a foreign company</h2>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #8a8a85; line-height: 1.5;">SK Hynix lists on Nasdaq with a $26.5B raise — a new record for foreign IPOs and a fresh AI-memory anchor for US investors.</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">What is it?</strong><br>
SK Hynix, the world's #2 memory maker and Nvidia's main HBM supplier, priced its US IPO of American Depositary Shares at $149. The offering raises about $26.5B and lists on Nasdaq under SKHYV, transitioning to SKHY for regular-way trading on Monday July 13.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">How does it work?</strong><br>
The pricing covers 177.9M ADSs (each = 1/10 of a Seoul common share). Books were ~7x oversubscribed, and Bloomberg reports it was the first US IPO priced at a premium to the home-market reference, eclipsing Alibaba's $25B 2014 debut.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Why does it matter?</strong><br>
Direct US access to the HBM/DRAM cycle draws forced buying across memory-adjacent names (MU +4.5%, SNDK +7.6%, SMH +2.5%). It also sets a precedent for future foreign-issuer mega listings and tests whether AI-memory demand can absorb another ~$26B of paper.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Who is it for?</strong><br>
Semis desks, IPO/allocations teams, and Nvidia supply-chain analysts tracking HBM capacity and pricing.
</p>

<h2 style="margin: 0 0 8px; font-size: 24px; font-weight: 800; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.2;">Meta (META) +~6% to ~$669.81 — leaked memo reveals custom 'Iris' AI chip enters production in September at ~$22B/GW, roughly half of BofA's $45B/GW estimate</h2>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #8a8a85; line-height: 1.5;">Leaked Meta memo says its AI build-out costs half of what Wall Street penciled in — and the custom 'Iris' chip tapes out in September.</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">What is it?</strong><br>
Reuters obtained an internal Meta memo showing the company will start production of its next in-house AI chip, code-named 'Iris', in September. It's the second-generation Meta Training and Inference Accelerator (MTIA), designed with Broadcom and manufactured at TSMC.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">How does it work?</strong><br>
Meta plans to add 5.5 GW of compute in H2 2026 and reach 14 GW total by end-2027. Cross-referenced against guided ~$145B of 2026 AI capex, that implies ~$22B per GW of compute build cost — roughly half the ~$45B/GW BofA had modeled. BofA reiterated Buy with an $835 PT after the memo hit.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Why does it matter?</strong><br>
The primary AI-capex bear case has been ROIC compression as hyperscalers overspend on GPUs. A $22B/GW build cost puts META's per-token economics closer to Google's TPU curve. Second-order: Broadcom (design partner) and TSMC (fab) are direct pull-throughs; NVDA loses a bit of TAM narrative for META-scale workloads.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Who is it for?</strong><br>
AI-capex analysts, hyperscaler traders, and semi supply-chain desks tracking custom silicon adoption vs. Nvidia GPU dependency.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #8a8a85; line-height: 1.5;">Delta beats on both lines, reinstates FY26 guide and hikes the dividend 15% — but shares slip on a record $4.4B fuel bill.</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">What is it?</strong><br>
Delta reported June-quarter 2026 results on Friday July 10 with adjusted EPS of $1.56 (beat $1.51 est) and adjusted revenue of $17.67B (+14% Y/Y, all-time record), driven by premium (+17%) and loyalty (+19%) revenue. Management reinstated the full-year adjusted EPS guide of $6.50-$7.50.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">How does it work?</strong><br>
Pre-tax profit was $1.4B despite $4.4B of fuel expense (+77% Y/Y, an all-time quarterly record) from the Q2 Brent/WTI spike. Delta also announced a 15% dividend increase and Q3 guidance of EPS $2.00-$2.50 and upper mid-teens revenue growth.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Why does it matter?</strong><br>
Delta's ability to reinstate FY26 guidance through a $4.4B fuel wall is the key read-through for UAL/AAL/LUV bulls. Stock trading -2.3% shows the market is fading on Q3 EPS coming in below some analysts' expectations despite the Q2 beat. Watch UAL's July 16 print.

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">

<h2 style="margin: 0 0 8px; font-size: 24px; font-weight: 800; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.2;">Micron (MU) +4.52% to $991.64 — lifts US chip investment to $250B+ through 2035, pours first concrete at Clay NY fab a quarter ahead of schedule; SOX +4.1%, AMD +7.7%</h2>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #8a8a85; line-height: 1.5;">Micron accelerates US chip plan to $250B+ through 2035, marks first concrete pour at Clay, NY fab a quarter ahead of schedule.</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">What is it?</strong><br>
Micron raised its total planned US semiconductor investment to more than $250B through 2035 (from $200B), coinciding with the first concrete pour at its Clay, NY fab — more than one quarter ahead of the original schedule, less than six months after the January 2026 groundbreaking.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">How does it work?</strong><br>
The plan funds up to four fabs at Clay, NY plus expansions in Idaho (first wafer output mid-2027) and Virginia. Targets: 40% of Micron's DRAM output produced in the US and 90,000+ direct-plus-indirect jobs. A separate $3B strategic investment targets the domestic semiconductor supply chain.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Why does it matter?</strong><br>
MU closed +4.52% at $991.64 as SOX rallied 4.1% and AMD ripped +7.7%. The scale formalises the HBM-sold-out-through-2026 backdrop Micron flagged at Q3 FY26 earnings and cements memory as a lead beneficiary of AI-era capex.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Who is it for?</strong><br>
Semis desks, memory-cycle traders, and US industrial-policy watchers tracking CHIPS Act execution and domestic semiconductor capacity buildout.
</p>

<h2 style="margin: 0 0 8px; font-size: 24px; font-weight: 800; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.2;">Ionis (IONS) -~20%, AstraZeneca (AZN) -9% — Phase 3 CARDIO-TTRansform of Wainua misses ATTR-CM primary endpoint; Alnylam (ALNY) +~18%, BridgeBio (BBIO) +~10%</h2>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #8a8a85; line-height: 1.5;">AstraZeneca/Ionis Wainua misses the Phase 3 ATTR-CM primary endpoint on top of standard care — silencer story reopens for Alnylam and BridgeBio.</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">What is it?</strong><br>
The CARDIO-TTRansform Phase 3 trial of Wainua (eplontersen) did not meet its primary endpoint — a composite of CV mortality and recurrent CV events at Week 140 — in adults with ATTR-CM. The trial enrolled 1,432 patients across 130 sites in 20 countries; full data will be presented at ESC Congress in August.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">How does it work?</strong><br>
57% of patients in each arm were already on a stabilizer (tafamidis or acoramidis) at baseline, and 24% initiated one during the trial. A monotherapy subgroup showed a nominally significant HR of 0.71, but the on-stabilizer subgroup showed no treatment effect — the stabilizer floor is now too high for the silencer to clear the endpoint.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Why does it matter?</strong><br>
Wainua's ATTR-CM label was the major 2027 catalyst for both IONS and AZN — it just walked. Alnylam gapped +~18% because Amvuttra (vutrisiran), already approved in ATTR-CM, loses its most credible near-term silencer competitor. BridgeBio's Attruby rallied ~10% as the stabilizer combination dominated the trial population.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Who is it for?</strong><br>
Biotech analysts, ATTR-CM prescribers, and holders of IONS, AZN, ALNY, and BBIO tracking the transthyretin amyloidosis competitive landscape.
</p>
