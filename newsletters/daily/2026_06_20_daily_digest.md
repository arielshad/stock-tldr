<h2 style="margin: 0 0 8px; font-size: 24px; font-weight: 800; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.2;">FOMC June 2026 — Warsh's Hawkish Hold</h2>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #8a8a85; line-height: 1.5;">Warsh's debut FOMC keeps rates on hold but the dot plot pivots from cuts to hikes as Iran-war energy inflation pushes 2026 PCE to 3.6%.</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">What is it?</strong><br>
The FOMC concluded its June 16–17 meeting — Kevin Warsh's first as Fed Chair — leaving the federal funds target range unchanged at 3.50–3.75% on a unanimous 12-0 vote. The accompanying Summary of Economic Projections delivered the hawkish surprise: the median 2026 year-end fed funds rate jumped to 3.8% from 3.4% in March, implying a 25bp hike rather than the cut previously penciled in.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">How does it work?</strong><br>
Nine of 18 participants now project at least one rate hike in 2026, with six expecting two 25bp hikes. The median PCE inflation projection was raised to 3.6%, reflecting the Iran-war energy shock; the statement dropped its cutting bias and added the line "The Committee will deliver price stability."
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Why does it matter?</strong><br>
This is the first hawkish pivot of the Warsh era. The 10Y Treasury yield rose ~6bp to ~4.49% and equities sold off sharply — S&P 500 -1.21%, Nasdaq -1.34%, Dow -0.98%. CME FedWatch now prices ~60% odds of at least one hike by year-end.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Who is it for?</strong><br>
Rates, macro and equity traders watching the Warsh-era policy path into PCE (June 27) and the July 28–29 FOMC.
</p>

<h2 style="margin: 0 0 8px; font-size: 24px; font-weight: 800; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.2;">U.S.–Iran Bürgenstock Talks Postponed</h2>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #8a8a85; line-height: 1.5;">Swiss FM confirms June 19 Bürgenstock signing postponed — Vance cancels trip, Iran cites digital MoU; oil bounces, Europe slips.</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">What is it?</strong><br>
Switzerland's Federal Department of Foreign Affairs confirmed on Friday June 19 that the first in-person round of U.S.–Iran technical talks scheduled at the Bürgenstock resort has been postponed until further notice. Iran's foreign-ministry spokesman said an in-person meeting was no longer urgent because the MoU text was signed digitally on June 18.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">How does it work?</strong><br>
VP JD Vance did not depart; the White House said "the plans for the upcoming technical talks have not been finalised." The postponement coincided with a Friday IDF strike on ~150 Hezbollah targets in Lebanon; a renewed ceasefire took effect at 4 p.m. local time.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Why does it matter?</strong><br>
Brent rebounded ~0.93% to $80.59 and WTI changed hands near $76.28 as the postponement reintroduced durability risk. Stoxx 600 fell 0.24%, FTSE 100 -0.35%, CAC 40 -0.55%; U.S. markets were closed for Juneteenth.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Who is it for?</strong><br>
Macro and energy desks tracking the Iran-deal implementation track and Hormuz throughput risk into Monday's U.S. cash open.
</p>

<h2 style="margin: 0 0 8px; font-size: 24px; font-weight: 800; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.2;">Bank of England Hawkish Hold at 3.75%</h2>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #8a8a85; line-height: 1.5;">A 7-2 hold with two voices already pulling for a hike — the BoE has joined Warsh's hawkish swing on the same day.</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">What is it?</strong><br>
At 12:00 noon GMT on Thursday June 18, the Bank of England's MPC voted to keep Bank Rate at 3.75% by a 7-2 vote. Chief Economist Huw Pill and external member Megan Greene preferred a 25bp HIKE to 4.00%, not a cut — with UK CPI running at 2.8% Y/Y.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">How does it work?</strong><br>
The MPC minutes flagged that energy effects from the Iran war are still feeding through and broader UK momentum is slowing. The decision landed less than 24 hours after the Fed's own SEP swung hawkish under Warsh's first FOMC.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Why does it matter?</strong><br>
Sterling fell sharply — GBP/USD dropped 0.58% to 1.3213, its weakest since March 13. Both central banks now contain hawkish dissents rather than dovish ones, a hard regime change for GBP carry trades and UK gilts.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Who is it for?</strong><br>
Macro traders, GBP/cable books, and gilts desks repricing the Fed-BoE policy divergence narrative.
</p>

<h2 style="margin: 0 0 8px; font-size: 24px; font-weight: 800; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.2;">Intel +9% on Trump's Apple Chip-Deal Claim</h2>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #8a8a85; line-height: 1.5;">Trump's Truth Social post lights a fire under INTC and the SOXX even as Apple and Intel stay silent on any signed deal.</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">What is it?</strong><br>
On Thursday morning June 18, President Trump posted on Truth Social that Apple has agreed to work with Intel to "design and build its Chips in America." Intel jumped roughly 9% in premarket trading to about $131.96, dragging the whole semi complex higher. Neither Apple nor Intel has issued a confirming statement.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">How does it work?</strong><br>
The claim follows a Wall Street Journal scoop in May that Intel and Apple had been in talks for nearly a year about Intel manufacturing some Apple-designed silicon. Intel already began pilot production of its 18A-P node last week at the VLSI Symposium, the most plausible node for any Apple-bound work.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Why does it matter?</strong><br>
Apple is the most coveted foundry customer in the world; even an unconfirmed pact reshapes the read on Intel Foundry's revenue trajectory and pressures TSMC's perceived monopoly. SOXX rallied 4.6% with MU +4.8%, AMD +3.9%, AVGO +3% in sympathy.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Who is it for?</strong><br>
Semis traders and US-manufacturing thematic books — this remains a Trump statement, not a confirmed deal, until either company files or issues a press release.
