<h2 style="margin: 0 0 8px; font-size: 24px; font-weight: 800; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.2;">Trump confirms U.S.–Iran deal 'now complete' — naval blockade ends, Strait of Hormuz reopens; WTI -4.8% to $80.80</h2>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #8a8a85; line-height: 1.5;">Trump declares the U.S.–Iran deal complete, orders the naval blockade lifted immediately and the Strait of Hormuz reopened; Iran confirms.</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">What is it?</strong><br>
Late Sunday June 14, President Trump posted on Truth Social that "The Deal with the Islamic Republic of Iran is now complete," authorizing the toll-free reopening of the Strait of Hormuz and immediate removal of the U.S. naval blockade. Pakistan PM Shehbaz Sharif confirmed the agreement, with the formal signing ceremony set for June 19 in Switzerland; Iran's deputy FM Kazem Gharibabadi said military operations end "immediately and permanently."
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">How does it work?</strong><br>
Per the MoU: the U.S. lifts its naval blockade and releases ~$25B in frozen Iranian assets while waiving oil-export sanctions; Iran commits to no new uranium enrichment and in-country dilution of its 440.9 kg of 60%-enriched stockpile. A 60-day window opens to negotiate a comprehensive final deal.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Why does it matter?</strong><br>
WTI (CL=F) fell 4.8% to $80.80 and Brent (BZ=F) dropped 3.9% to $83.89 as the four-month Hormuz war-risk premium unwound; S&P 500 futures rose +0.97%, Nasdaq futures +1.67%, and Russell 2000 futures hit a record high. The deal lands directly ahead of the G7 in Évian (June 15–17) and Warsh's first FOMC meeting (June 16–17).
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Who is it for?</strong><br>
Macro / oil / FX / equity-index desks tracking the Hormuz reopening and its downstream effects on energy pricing and risk appetite.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">

</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Why does it matter?</strong><br>
SPCX instantly joins the top-5 U.S. listings by market cap alongside AAPL, MSFT, NVDA and GOOG. The 19% day-one pop re-opens the mega-IPO window after a quiet 2025 and lifts the pipeline read for filed names like Cerebras and Klarna.

<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Who is it for?</strong><br>
IPO desks, mega-cap allocators, and space sector investors tracking index-inclusion mechanics (Nasdaq-100 has a 90-day seasoning rule) and the greenshoe exercise window through ~July 11.

<h2 style="margin: 0 0 8px; font-size: 24px; font-weight: 800; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.2;">U-Mich June 2026 preliminary: sentiment 48.9 vs 46.0 est — first monthly rise in four months; 5y inflation expectations fall to 3.4%</h2>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #8a8a85; line-height: 1.5;">Sentiment ticks up off the record floor as gasoline relief and Iran de-escalation push 1-year inflation expectations back to 4.6%.</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">What is it?</strong><br>
U-Mich's preliminary June 2026 Index of Consumer Sentiment rose to 48.9 from May's record-low final of 44.8 — the first monthly increase in four months and a clear beat vs. the 46.0 consensus. Year-ahead inflation expectations fell to 4.6% (from 4.8%) and 5–10y long-run expectations fell to 3.4% (from 3.9%).
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">How does it work?</strong><br>
Survey director Joanne Hsu attributed the bounce to "early-month easing in gasoline prices" driven by Iran de-escalation; Current Conditions rose to 48.4 (from 45.8) and the Expectations sub-index jumped to 49.3 (from 44.1). Improvement was broad across age, income, education, and political affiliation.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Why does it matter?</strong><br>
The 5–10y long-run inflation expectations drop from 3.9% to 3.4% is the signal the Fed watches most — partially unwinding the spike that pushed May to a record low. Still, at 48.9 this is the second-lowest sentiment reading in series history since 1952, leaving the macro picture fragile heading into Warsh's first FOMC on June 16–17.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Who is it for?</strong><br>
Macro traders, FOMC watchers, and rates desks tracking the intersection of consumer inflation expectations and the Fed's reaction function.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">What is it?</strong><br>

</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">How does it work?</strong><br>
The combined entity would control HBO Max, CNN, Comedy Central, Nickelodeon, and the Paramount and Warner Bros studio labels; Paramount Skydance outbid Netflix's $83B offer to win Warner. <a href="https://finance.yahoo.com/quote/PSKY" style="color: #f7ff00;">PSKY</a> rose 2.77% to $10.62 and <a href="https://finance.yahoo.com/quote/WBD" style="color: #f7ff00;">WBD</a> rose 0.45% to $26.98; the tender-offer expiration was extended to July 1.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Why does it matter?</strong><br>
California AG Rob Bonta confirmed the state investigation remains active, and Senator Warren called on state AGs to block the deal — the multi-state antitrust action (California + New York) is now the binding constraint. WBD spread compression depends entirely on the state AG outcome, not Washington.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Who is it for?</strong><br>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #8a8a85; line-height: 1.5;">Dana merges with Eaton's Mobility business in a $5.1B Reverse Morris Trust — combined company targets $11B sales and $250M synergies, but DAN -12.7% on Friday as the math gets digested.</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">What is it?</strong><br>
Dana (NYSE: <a href="https://finance.yahoo.com/quote/DAN" style="color: #f7ff00;">DAN</a>) and Eaton (NYSE: <a href="https://finance.yahoo.com/quote/ETN" style="color: #f7ff00;">ETN</a>) announced a Reverse Morris Trust combination valued at ~$5.1B to merge Eaton's Mobility Group with Dana, creating a global powertrain company targeting ~$11B in pro forma 2026E sales and ~$1.7B adj EBITDA. Eaton holders own ≥50.1% of the combined company; Dana holders ~49.9%.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">How does it work?</strong><br>
Eaton spins or splits off its Mobility Group to Eaton shareholders, who then merge with Dana; Eaton receives a $1.1B cash distribution in the process. The $5.1B valuation equals ~8.3x 2026E adj EBITDA pre-synergies (5.9x with $250M run-rate synergies expected within 24 months); deal closes Q1 2027 subject to Dana shareholder vote.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Why does it matter?</strong><br>
DAN fell 12.7% to $30.96 as the market priced effective per-share dilution and 24-month integration execution risk; ETN ticked +1.2% to $380.01 as the divestiture sharpens its portfolio into a pure-play electrical/industrial company. Dana's 2030 targets jump to $14–$15B in sales and ~18% adj EBITDA margin.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Who is it for?</strong><br>
Auto-supplier analysts, industrials event-driven funds, and RMT/spin-off arbs modeling the close timeline and synergy ramp through Q1 2027.

<h2 style="margin: 0 0 8px; font-size: 24px; font-weight: 800; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.2;">Lennar Q2 FY26: $1.24 EPS / $7.9B revenue clear lowered bar, but gross margin slips to 15.6%; FY26 deliveries trimmed — LEN -4.5%</h2>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #8a8a85; line-height: 1.5;">Lennar clears a lowered Q2 FY26 bar but margins keep compressing as mortgage rates and incentives bite — FY26 delivery target trimmed and LEN -4.5% on Friday.</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">What is it?</strong><br>
Lennar (NYSE: <a href="https://finance.yahoo.com/quote/LEN" style="color: #f7ff00;">LEN</a>), the second-largest U.S. homebuilder, reported Q2 FY26 results with $1.24 EPS GAAP ($1.31 ex-MTM) on $7.9B in revenue — beating the lowered bar — but gross margin on home sales compressed to 15.6% from 17.8% Y/Y as average sales price fell 5% to $371K with incentives running at 12.9% of ASP.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">How does it work?</strong><br>
Deliveries rose 2% Y/Y to 20,519 homes (inside guidance) while new orders fell 4% to 21,749; Lennar repurchased 5M shares for $447M and ended the quarter with $1.8B cash. Q3 guidance calls for 20,500–21,500 deliveries at ~16% gross margin and ASP of $375K–$380K.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Why does it matter?</strong><br>
CEO Stuart Miller cited "persistently elevated mortgage rates, constrained affordability, and cautious consumer sentiment" compounded by a 4.2% May CPI print; FY26 full-year delivery target was trimmed to 82–83K homes. LEN fell 4.52% to $93.12 on Friday, dragging homebuilder comps <a href="https://finance.yahoo.com/quote/DHI" style="color: #f7ff00;">DHI</a> and <a href="https://finance.yahoo.com/quote/PHM" style="color: #f7ff00;">PHM</a> lower.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Who is it for?</strong><br>
Homebuilder analysts, rate-sensitive equity desks, and REIT / housing-data traders watching the FOMC meeting (June 16–17) for any signal on mortgage rate trajectory.
</p>
