<h2 style="margin: 0 0 8px; font-size: 24px; font-weight: 800; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.2;">Netflix (NFLX) Q2 2026 — beat by a hair, Q3 guide misses, stock –8% AH</h2>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #8a8a85; line-height: 1.5;">Netflix beat Q2 by a hair but a soft Q3 revenue guide sinks NFLX ~8% after-hours to a ~10-month low.</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">What is it?</strong><br>
Netflix reported Q2 FY26 revenue of $12.56B (+13.4% Y/Y) and diluted EPS of $0.80 — both narrowly beating guidance and Street estimates. The Q3 revenue outlook of $12.86B (+11.7% Y/Y) undershot analyst estimates that had drifted to ~$13B.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">How does it work?</strong><br>
Q2 operating margin came in at 33.4%, ahead of the 33% guide. FY26 revenue range was narrowed to $51.0–$51.4B with op margin still targeted at 31.5%. Q3 forecast: revenue $12.86B, EPS $0.82, free cash flow for Q2 was $1.53B.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Why does it matter?</strong><br>
NFLX slid ~8% AH to ~$68.28, the lowest since September 2025. Revenue growth is decelerating every quarter: 17.6% (Q4'25) → 16.2% (Q1'26) → 13.4% (Q2'26) → 11.7% (Q3'26 guide). Read-across into DIS, WBD, and PARA is negative.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Who is it for?</strong><br>
Streaming and AdTech desks, momentum-tech portfolio managers tracking the FAANG growth deceleration trade.
</p>

<h2 style="margin: 0 0 8px; font-size: 24px; font-weight: 800; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.2;">ABB agrees $5.5B all-cash takeover of UK's Rotork at 73% premium — biggest deal ever for the Swiss group</h2>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #8a8a85; line-height: 1.5;">ABB agrees to buy UK flow-control specialist Rotork for $5.5B all-cash at a 73% premium — the Swiss group's biggest deal ever.</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">What is it?</strong><br>
Swiss engineering group ABB agreed on July 16 to acquire Bath-based Rotork at 503p cash + 3p permitted interim dividend = 506p per share, valuing Rotork's fully-diluted equity at £4.136B (~$5.5B) — ABB's largest acquisition ever.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">How does it work?</strong><br>
The deal is structured as a court-sanctioned scheme of arrangement via ABB Automation Holding UK Limited. The 506p offer is a 73% premium to Rotork's July 15 close and ~60% to the 3-month VWAP. ABB funds the deal from $4.8B of proceeds from its October 2025 sale of the robotics division to SoftBank; completion is targeted H1 2027.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Why does it matter?</strong><br>
Rotork surged ~67% intraday to ~485p — one of the biggest single-day mid-cap pops of 2026. The deal adds ~3% to ABB group revenue and is immediately Op EBITA margin-accretive, part of a broader wave of foreign bids for UK-listed companies.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Who is it for?</strong><br>
European industrials M&A desks, UK special-situations traders, and merger-arbitrage funds tracking the scheme timeline through H1 2027.
</p>

AAPL closed at $327.50 (+$12.64, ~+4%), a fresh all-time closing high and the 15th intraday record of 2026. Alibaba ADRs (BABA) rose 4%+ on the same catalyst.
