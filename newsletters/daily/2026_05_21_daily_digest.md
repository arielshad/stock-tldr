<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #8a8a85; line-height: 1.5;">NVDA prints record $81.6B with Data Center up 92% — and adds an $80B buyback plus a 25× dividend hike — but stock barely moves AH.</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">What is it?</strong><br>
NVIDIA reported Q1 fiscal 2027 results for the quarter ended April 26, 2026. Revenue hit a record $81.6 billion, up 85% year-over-year, with Data Center at $75.2 billion (+92% Y/Y). The board added $80B to the buyback authorization and lifted the quarterly dividend from $0.01 to $0.25 per share.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">How does it work?</strong><br>
Non-GAAP EPS came in at $1.87 vs $1.77 consensus; GAAP EPS was $2.39. Q2 FY27 revenue is guided to $91.0B ±2%, above the ~$87.4B Street estimate, with no Data Center compute revenue from China assumed. NVIDIA also unveiled the Vera Rubin platform and BlueField-4 STX.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Why does it matter?</strong><br>
The most-watched print of the quarter cleared a high bar — a beat-and-raise, a massive capital-return announcement, and an explicit guide ex-China. Despite that, NVDA seesawed to roughly -0.7% in extended trading as Interactive Brokers noted good news is already "baked in." Read-through extends to AI capex names (AVGO, ASML, AMD) and the broader SMH/DRAM complex.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Who is it for?</strong><br>
AI/semis traders, mega-cap factor desks, options-vol watchers.

<h2 style="margin: 0 0 8px; font-size: 24px; font-weight: 800; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.2;">US 30-year Treasury yield tops 5.197% — highest since July 2007; S&amp;P 500 -0.67% on Iran inflation fears</h2>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #8a8a85; line-height: 1.5;">Long-end Treasuries break to a 19-year high as the Iran-war oil shock seeps into the inflation outlook.</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">What is it?</strong><br>
The 30-year US Treasury yield briefly hit 5.197% on May 19 — its highest level since July 2007, before the global financial crisis. The 10-year yield touched 4.687%, the most since January 2025. The S&amp;P 500 closed -0.67% at 7,353.61 for its third straight losing session.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">How does it work?</strong><br>
Yields rose six basis points intraday on the long end as traders dumped duration over Iran-war energy spillovers and a widening fiscal deficit. With the Strait of Hormuz effectively closed, oil and gas prices are at four-year highs, now showing up in food and airfares. Citi flagged 5.5% as the next key level if the selloff extends.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Why does it matter?</strong><br>
Higher long yields raise mortgage, credit-card, and corporate borrowing costs while pressing equity multiples. Rate-sensitive groups — homebuilders, REITs, regional banks, megacap tech — carry the biggest markdown risk on each fresh leg higher. The NVDA earnings print and upcoming FOMC dynamics are now the key swing factors.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Who is it for?</strong><br>
Rates traders, allocators, equity duration desks.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #8a8a85; line-height: 1.5;">Powell's farewell meeting set the table for Warsh: four dissents — one dove, three hawks — and split on whether 'easing bias' even belongs in the statement.</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">What is it?</strong><br>
The Fed released minutes of the April 28-29, 2026 FOMC meeting, showing an 8-4 vote to hold the federal funds target range at 3.50-3.75%. It was Chair Powell's final meeting — his term expired May 15, with Warsh sworn in the same day.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">How does it work?</strong><br>
Governor Miran dissented to favor a 25bp cut, citing "overly restrictive" policy. Presidents Hammack, Kashkari, and Logan dissented against keeping easing-bias language in the statement — the four-dissent total is the most at any FOMC since October 1992. Discussion flagged the Middle East conflict (Iran) as a major source of economic uncertainty alongside persistent CPI/PPI pressure.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Why does it matter?</strong><br>
The minutes map who Warsh inherits and what battle lines look like into the June 16-17 meeting. Three hawkish dissenters on language suggests the path of least resistance is to hold, not cut, despite Trump's pressure campaign. The 30Y at a 19-year high near 5.20% reads any "easing bias" shift as inflationary.
</p>

<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Who is it for?</strong><br>
