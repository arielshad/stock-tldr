<h2 style="margin: 0 0 8px; font-size: 24px; font-weight: 800; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.2;">Marvell ~+25% premarket on June 2 as Jensen Huang calls MRVL 'the next trillion-dollar company' at Computex 2026</h2>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #8a8a85; line-height: 1.5;">Huang puts Marvell in the trillion-dollar conversation at Computex; MRVL ~+25% premarket.</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">What is it?</strong><br>
At Computex 2026 in Taipei on June 2, NVIDIA CEO Jensen Huang appeared on stage alongside Marvell CEO Matt Murphy and described Marvell as "the next trillion-dollar company." MRVL closed Friday near $174.6 (market cap ~$192B) and surged about 25% in U.S. premarket trading to roughly $219.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">How does it work?</strong><br>
Huang's pitch: Marvell's networking and connectivity silicon is essential to AI data centers where compute is spread across thousands of GPUs. NVIDIA took a $2B stake in Marvell three months ago, and the two companies are tightening NVLink Fusion integration so Marvell's custom silicon can plug directly into NVIDIA AI fabrics.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Why does it matter?</strong><br>
A trillion-dollar market cap from here implies roughly a 5x re-rating — the comment alone added more than $40B of market value in premarket. It cements Marvell as NVIDIA's preferred custom-silicon partner and pulls the broader AI-networking complex (AVGO, ANET, COHR) along for the read-through.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Who is it for?</strong><br>
AI infrastructure traders watching the custom-silicon and networking stack adjacent to NVIDIA.
</p>

</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #8a8a85; line-height: 1.5;">Goldman halves its Intuit price target and goes outright Sell on AI tax-prep disruption.</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">What is it?</strong><br>
Goldman Sachs downgraded Intuit from Neutral to Sell on June 2 and slashed its 12-month price target to $276 from $519, citing rising competitive threats from AI-powered tax-preparation entrants. INTU traded ~-6% in premarket.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">How does it work?</strong><br>
Goldman flagged TurboTax (~25% of Intuit revenue) as most exposed, pointing to Prime Meridian, Perplexity Tax, and Chime Tax as AI models that can process returns at near-zero marginal cost vs TurboTax's ~$162 average revenue per return. The bank reset valuation to 15x GAAP earnings.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Why does it matter?</strong><br>
The PT cut roughly halves Goldman's prior fair value and follows Intuit's May 20 print, which included a 17% workforce cut and Anthropic + OpenAI partnerships. It is one of the clearest Street statements that AI tooling is now compressing legacy workflow SaaS moats — read-through to ADBE, ADP, and CRM.
</p>

<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Who is it for?</strong><br>
Fintech and SaaS long/short traders watching AI-disruption repricing across horizontal software.
