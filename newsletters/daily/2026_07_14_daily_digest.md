<h2 style="margin: 0 0 8px; font-size: 24px; font-weight: 800; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.2;">Trump reinstates Iranian blockade and imposes 20% Strait of Hormuz cargo toll — Brent +9.6% to $83.30, biggest daily gain since May 2020</h2>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #8a8a85; line-height: 1.5;">Trump reinstates Iran blockade and demands a 20% cut of every Hormuz cargo; oil posts its biggest daily jump in six years.</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">What is it?</strong><br>
President Trump announced on Truth Social that the U.S. is reinstating a naval blockade on Iranian ships and demanding 20% of the value of all cargo transiting the Strait of Hormuz as a "reimbursement" for U.S. protection. The blockade takes effect Tuesday July 14 at 2000 GMT and covers Iran's entire coastline, ports, and oil terminals regardless of vessel flag.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">How does it work?</strong><br>
Non-Iranian ships pay 20% of cargo value for passage — roughly $32M per supertanker vs. Iran's prior ~$2M toll. The IMO Secretary-General said there is "no legal basis" for charging fees on an international strait, but CENTCOM confirmed the blockade is operational.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Why does it matter?</strong><br>
Brent closed +9.6% at $83.30/bbl — its biggest single-day gain since May 2020 — and WTI closed +9.4% at $78.14, putting global oil, LNG, and container traffic back on war-risk footing hours before Q2 U.S. bank earnings and the June CPI print on Tuesday.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Who is it for?</strong><br>
Energy desks, macro traders, and tanker equity holders tracking the renewed Hormuz risk premium. The 20% toll erases the toll-free concession Trump signed on June 14.
</p>

<h2 style="margin: 0 0 8px; font-size: 24px; font-weight: 800; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.2;">KOSPI −8.95% triggers year's 7th circuit breaker — SK Hynix posts record −15.37% drop, Samsung −10.7%</h2>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #8a8a85; line-height: 1.5;">KOSPI craters 8.95% in the year's 7th circuit-breaker session as SK Hynix logs its worst day on record and Samsung sheds 10.7%.</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">What is it?</strong><br>
South Korea's KOSPI closed 8.95% lower at 6,806.93 on Monday July 13 after Korea Exchange activated a Level 1 circuit breaker at 1:28 pm KST, freezing trading for 20 minutes — the 7th halt of 2026. SK Hynix (000660.KS) plunged a record 15.37% to ₩1,845,000 and Samsung Electronics fell 10.7% to ₩254,500.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">How does it work?</strong><br>
Three catalysts stacked simultaneously: profit-taking after SK Hynix's record $26.5B Nasdaq IPO on July 10, analyst disappointment over HBM4 shipment volumes not ramping as expected, and the Hormuz oil spike that hit the Korean won and broad risk sentiment.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Why does it matter?</strong><br>
The AI-memory trade drove KOSPI's biggest 2026 gains, so a record drop in SK Hynix and a 10%+ Samsung move represents a broad derating of that theme. The SKHY ADR now trades at a ~37% premium to its Seoul shares — an arb window traders will watch narrow all week. US memory peers followed lower: MU −6%, SNDK −10%.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Who is it for?</strong><br>
Memory and AI-hardware traders, KRW/oil cross-asset desks, and SKHY arb watchers tracking the Seoul-to-Nasdaq premium.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #8a8a85; line-height: 1.5;">Broadest single-night exchange of the war: US bombs ~140 Iranian sites, Iran hits 6 GCC states, oil gaps up 3.2% Sunday.</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">What is it?</strong><br>
The US and Iran traded their heaviest overnight strikes on Saturday night into Sunday July 12. CENTCOM hit roughly 140 Iranian military targets — missile and drone launch sites, ammunition dumps, IRGC fast boats, and facilities near Bandar Abbas and Qeshm Island. Iran's IRGC then fired ballistic missiles and drones across Qatar, UAE, Kuwait, Bahrain, Oman, and Jordan.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">How does it work?</strong><br>
IRGC strikes targeted Al Udeid Air Base in Qatar (the largest US installation in the Middle East), a Kuwait Oil Company offshore drilling platform (1 worker injured, substantial rig damage), Prince Hassan Air Base in Jordan, and US-affiliated infrastructure in Oman and the UAE. Three Qatar civilians were hurt by Patriot interception shrapnel. Iran's Supreme Leader Mojtaba Khamenei vowed further retaliation.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Why does it matter?</strong><br>
Sunday futures showed WTI +3.2% to $73.70 and Brent +3.2% to $78.45; Rapidan Energy's Bob McNally warned of "a lot of complacency" as only 22 tankers transited Hormuz that week vs. a ~33/day norm. GCC unity hardened — all six targeted governments formally condemned the strikes, raising the political cost of any further Iranian volley.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Who is it for?</strong><br>
Macro, oil, defense, and geopolitical-risk desks — and anyone positioned ahead of June CPI on Tuesday July 14 and Q2 big-bank earnings (JPM, WFC, C, GS, BAC on July 14–16).
</p>

<h2 style="margin: 0 0 8px; font-size: 24px; font-weight: 800; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.2;">SK Hynix (SKHY) Nasdaq debut — 177.9M ADSs priced at $149 raise $26.5B, the largest ever US IPO by a foreign company</h2>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #8a8a85; line-height: 1.5;">SK Hynix lists on Nasdaq with a $26.5B raise — a new record for foreign IPOs and a fresh AI-memory anchor for US investors.</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">What is it?</strong><br>
SK Hynix, the world's #2 memory maker and Nvidia's main high-bandwidth-memory (HBM) supplier, priced 177.9M ADSs at $149 on Nasdaq, raising ~$26.5B. The offering tops Alibaba's $25B 2014 record for the largest ever US IPO by a foreign company; books were approximately 7× oversubscribed.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">How does it work?</strong><br>
Each ADS represents 1/10 of a Seoul common share; SKHYV traded when-issued on July 10 with regular-way SKHY trading opening July 13. Bloomberg reported it was the first US IPO priced at a premium to the home-market reference, with the direct listing giving US investors clean HBM/DRAM exposure without Korean withholding tax drag.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Why does it matter?</strong><br>
The listing ripped memory-adjacent names on debut day (MU +4.5%, SNDK +7.6%, SMH +2.5%), but the Seoul shares subsequently fell a record 15.37% on July 13 as the ADR premium stretched to ~37% and Hormuz escalation hit Korean risk sentiment. QQQ/NDX index-inclusion buying is expected in the ~15-day fast-track window.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Who is it for?</strong><br>
Semis desks, IPO allocations teams, Nvidia supply-chain analysts, and index fund managers who need to size the forced-buying window across QQQ, NDX, and SMH rebalances.
</p>

</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Why does it matter?</strong><br>

META closed +6.07% to ~$669.81; BofA reiterated Buy with an $835 price target. Broadcom and TSMC are direct pull-throughs; Nvidia loses some TAM narrative for Meta-scale workloads.
</p>

<h2 style="margin: 0 0 8px; font-size: 24px; font-weight: 800; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.2;">Circle (CRCL) +4.97% to $66.14 — OCC grants first national trust bank charter for a US stablecoin issuer; intraday high $72.85 (+15.6%)</h2>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #8a8a85; line-height: 1.5;">Circle wins the first OCC national trust charter for a US stablecoin issuer, unlocking federally supervised USDC custody.</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">What is it?</strong><br>
The OCC granted Circle final approval on July 10 to establish First National Digital Currency Bank, N.A. (operating as Circle National Trust) — the first federal trust charter awarded to a US stablecoin issuer, converting Circle from a state-regulated operator into a directly OCC-supervised national trust bank.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">How does it work?</strong><br>
Circle filed in June 2025 and received conditional approval in December; the trust will initially provide fiduciary digital asset custody for Circle and affiliates before expanding to institutional clients such as banks. Reserve management for USDC ($73.2B in circulation) is designed as a future capability but is not yet live.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Why does it matter?</strong><br>
The charter reduces Circle's counterparty dependence on third-party custody banks and puts it ahead of Ripple, Paxos, and BitGo — which hold only conditional approvals — in the digital asset banking race. CRCL popped +15.6% intraday to $72.85 before fading to a +4.97% close at $66.14 as Mizuho flagged the rally as "overly optimistic" vs. rising Stripe/OpenUSD competition.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Who is it for?</strong><br>
Stablecoin desks, crypto ETF flow watchers, and financial-regulation trackers following the digital asset custody race and the long-term path to USDC reserve custody under OCC supervision.
</p>
