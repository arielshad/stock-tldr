<h2 style="margin: 0 0 8px; font-size: 24px; font-weight: 800; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.2;">June CPI -0.4% M/M, +3.5% Y/Y — biggest monthly headline drop in 6+ years</h2>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #8a8a85; line-height: 1.5;">June CPI prints its biggest monthly headline decline in over six years, but Warsh's Fed still expected to hold in July and hike in September.</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">What is it?</strong><br>
The BLS released the June 2026 CPI at 8:30 a.m. ET: headline fell 0.4% M/M SA, dragging the annual rate to 3.5% from May's 4.2%. Core CPI was flat on the month and softened to +2.6% Y/Y — the lowest core annual rate in over a year.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">How does it work?</strong><br>
Dow Jones consensus was headline -0.2% M/M / +3.8% Y/Y and core +0.2% M/M / +2.9% Y/Y — both prints beat across the board. The energy index dropped 5.7% M/M (gasoline and fuel oil each -9%+) as the Iran ceasefire-era oil unwind filtered through; shelter rose only 0.1% M/M, its coolest print all year.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Why does it matter?</strong><br>
Even with the soft print, futures markets held bets that Warsh's Fed stays on hold July 28-29 and then hikes 25bp in September to 3.75-4.00%. The monthly headline drop is the largest since April 2020, but the Iran-Hormuz blockade that began July 13 could reverse the energy tailwind next month.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Who is it for?</strong><br>
Macro traders, rates desks, and Fed watchers parsing the July-to-September FOMC path.
</p>

<h2 style="margin: 0 0 8px; font-size: 24px; font-weight: 800; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.2;">Warsh's House debut: "no tolerance for persistently elevated inflation" — 90 minutes after a soft CPI print</h2>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #8a8a85; line-height: 1.5;">Warsh's first Congressional testimony leans on "no tolerance" inflation language and teases five task-force review of Fed practices.</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">What is it?</strong><br>
Fed Chair Kevin Warsh delivered the Semiannual Monetary Policy Report to the House Financial Services Committee at 10:00 a.m. ET on July 14 — his first Capitol Hill appearance since being sworn in May 22. Senate Banking follows Wednesday July 15 at 10:00 a.m. ET.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">How does it work?</strong><br>
Warsh reiterated the fed funds target at 3.50-3.75%, framed the Fed at "a hinge point in history," and announced five task forces reviewing communications, balance-sheet policy, data sources, productivity/jobs, and inflation frameworks — the most concrete institutional-review pivot from the Powell era to date.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Why does it matter?</strong><br>
The "no tolerance" framing held even after a below-consensus CPI, confirming the market's July-hold / September-25bp-hike pricing. Warsh also flagged "high-tech spending logged an especially impressive growth rate of nearly 25 percent" — a nod to AI capex that carries forward from the June SEP.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Who is it for?</strong><br>
Fed watchers, rates traders, and macro desks watching the July 28-29 FOMC decision.

<h2 style="margin: 0 0 8px; font-size: 24px; font-weight: 800; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.2;">Citi Q2 best in a decade: EPS $3.15 crushes $2.73 est, $30B buyback launched, 12% dividend hike planned</h2>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #8a8a85; line-height: 1.5;">Citi's best quarter in a decade — EPS $3.15 crushes $2.73 est on $24.8B revenue as Fraser launches a $30B buyback and 12% dividend hike.</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">What is it?</strong><br>
Citigroup reported Q2 2026 results before the bell on July 14. Net income was $5.8B (+45% Y/Y) or $3.15 per diluted share, versus $2.73 consensus and $1.96 a year ago. Revenue of $24.8B (+14% Y/Y) was Citi's best quarterly print in a decade.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">How does it work?</strong><br>
All five businesses grew: Markets +17% (Equities +45%, prime balances +60%), Banking +34%, Services delivered record revenue at &gt;30% return, and Wealth posted its 9th consecutive quarter of growth. The efficiency ratio dropped ~530bp Y/Y to 57.4%. Fraser announced a $30B buyback authorization and a planned 12% dividend increase, returning ~$5B in Q2.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Why does it matter?</strong><br>
This print validates Fraser's five-business restructure — every business grew double-digits, RoTCE stepped from 8.7% to 13.0% Y/Y, and Citi joined the $30B-buyback cohort. Despite the beat, C shares faded ~4% intraday on "no guidance raise, more investment spend" commentary.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Who is it for?</strong><br>
Bank analysts, financials investors, and restructuring / capital-return traders watching Citi's RoTCE path.
</p>

<h2 style="margin: 0 0 8px; font-size: 24px; font-weight: 800; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.2;">IBM craters ~25% — worst single day in its history — after CEO Krishna letter preannounces Q2 miss</h2>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #8a8a85; line-height: 1.5;">IBM preannounces a Q2 miss on Krishna letter; stock craters ~25% for worst single day in IBM history.</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">What is it?</strong><br>
IBM CEO Arvind Krishna published a letter to investors at 7:05 a.m. ET on July 14 disclosing preliminary Q2 2026 results ahead of the July 22 earnings call. Revenue came in at $17.2B (+1% Y/Y) versus the $17.85B Street consensus, and non-GAAP EPS was $2.93 versus the $3.01 estimate.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">How does it work?</strong><br>
Software grew +5% (Red Hat accelerated to +11%) but Infrastructure -7% and Consulting was flat. In the final weeks of June, clients redirected capex toward supply-constrained servers/storage ahead of price increases, industry-wide cybersecurity concerns distracted buyers, and "numerous large deals failed to close on the timelines we expected," per Krishna.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Why does it matter?</strong><br>
IBM shares dropped ~25% intraday — the worst single session in IBM's history, sharper than the 1987 Black Monday drop. The Dow closed flat (+0.02%) only because IBM stripped ~150-200 points off. The "AI capex eating enterprise IT budgets" narrative now bites the classic software-and-consulting cohort (ACN, INFY, EPAM traded weaker on the readthrough).
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Who is it for?</strong><br>
Enterprise software analysts, IT-services sell-side, and Dow index traders who need to understand the AI-budget displacement dynamic.
</p>
