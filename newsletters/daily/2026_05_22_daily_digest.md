<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #8a8a85; line-height: 1.5;">Washington's first big-money quantum bet: $2B in CHIPS Letters of Intent across 9 firms — and Uncle Sam takes minority equity in each.</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">What is it?</strong><br>
On May 21, the U.S. Department of Commerce announced Letters of Intent with nine American quantum-computing companies for $2.013B in CHIPS Act incentives. The anchor award is a proposed $1B to IBM to build 'Anderon', a new 300mm quantum wafer foundry in Albany, NY (IBM matches $1B in cash).
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">How does it work?</strong><br>
Other awards: GlobalFoundries $375M; $100M each to Atom Computing, D-Wave, Infleqtion, PsiQuantum, Quantinuum, and Rigetti; Diraq $38M. As a condition of receiving funds, Commerce takes a minority non-controlling equity stake in each company — mirroring the Intel CHIPS-equity precedent.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Why does it matter?</strong><br>
This is the first federal-scale industrial policy for U.S. quantum, turning a sub-sector that traded mostly on press-release tone into a CHIPS Act beneficiary. RGTI ripped ~23%, QBTS ~20%, IBM +6.2%, and IONQ +6.9% premarket despite not being on the award list.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Who is it for?</strong><br>
Policy desks, semis/AI infrastructure investors, quantum specialists.
</p>
