<h2 style="margin: 0 0 8px; font-size: 24px; font-weight: 800; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.2;">Apple (AAPL) closes at record $327.50 (+4%) after China clears Apple Intelligence with Alibaba's Qwen — market cap approaches $5T</h2>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #8a8a85; line-height: 1.5;">China clears Apple Intelligence with Alibaba's Qwen, and Apple prints an all-time closing high at $327.50 — market cap now within ~$200B of $5T.</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">What is it?</strong><br>
China's Cyberspace Administration approved Apple Intelligence as one of seven on-device gen-AI services for smartphones, with Alibaba's Tongyi Qwen model powering it across iOS, iPadOS, macOS and visionOS in mainland China. AAPL closed at a fresh all-time closing high of $327.50 on July 15 — the 15th intraday record of 2026.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">How does it work?</strong><br>
Chinese law requires LLM providers to register with the CAC before public deployment. The approval unlocks Apple Intelligence for Chinese users, replacing OpenAI's GPT (used outside China) with Alibaba's Qwen — no specific launch date was given. Alibaba's US ADRs (BABA) also rose over 4% on the same catalyst.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Why does it matter?</strong><br>
China is Apple's third-largest revenue region and the one meaningful market where Apple Intelligence had been locked out — clearance hands Apple a fresh AI-marketing hook for the next iPhone cycle in the world's largest smartphone market. At $327.50, AAPL's market cap is ~$4.81T, roughly $200B behind Nvidia's $5T.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Who is it for?</strong><br>
Mega-cap tech traders, China consumer desks, AI supply-chain analysts.
</p>

<h2 style="margin: 0 0 8px; font-size: 24px; font-weight: 800; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.2;">PayPal (PYPL) reportedly draws $53B takeover offer from Stripe and Advent at $60.50/sh cash — 28% premium, PYPL +18-20% premarket</h2>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #8a8a85; line-height: 1.5;">Stripe and Advent make an unsolicited $60.50-per-share cash bid for PayPal — 28% premium, ~$53B deal, ~$50B of committed bank financing already lined up.</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">What is it?</strong><br>
Reuters reported on July 15 that private-equity firm Advent International and privately-held Stripe jointly offered $60.50 per share in cash to acquire PayPal, valuing it at more than $53 billion — a 28% premium to Tuesday's close. The initial approach was made in April; the two firms are now pushing PayPal to respond over the next several weeks.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">How does it work?</strong><br>
The bid carries ~$50B in committed bank financing, with Stripe and Advent sharing ownership 50/50 and no plans to dismantle the company. PayPal has not responded publicly; PYPL was up 18-20% premarket on the Reuters scoop.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Why does it matter?</strong><br>
PayPal peaked near a $360B market cap in 2021 and had cratered to ~$36B pre-bid as Apple Pay, Google Pay and embedded wallets eroded its franchise. A private-market take-out with Stripe as co-owner would rerate the entire payments tape (V, MA, SQ) and likely invite competing bids.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Who is it for?</strong><br>
Fintech investors, M&amp;A arb desks, payments analysts.
</p>
