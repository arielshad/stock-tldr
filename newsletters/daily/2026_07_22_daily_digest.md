<h2 style="margin: 0 0 8px; font-size: 24px; font-weight: 800; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.2;">Trump Imposes 50% Tariff on Canadian Autos, Alcohol and Dairy Under Section 338 — ~$20B of Goods, Effective in 30 Days</h2>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #8a8a85; line-height: 1.5;">Three proclamations signed July 20 under the Tariff Act of 1930 target ~$20B of Canadian imports; oil, potash, fish, critical minerals, and existing Section 232 steel/auto duties are excluded.</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">What is it?</strong><br>
President Trump signed three proclamations on July 20 levying a 50% tariff on Canadian autos, alcohol, and dairy under Section 338 of the Tariff Act of 1930 — a rarely used provision allowing duties up to 50% on countries the President finds discriminating against U.S. commerce. The action follows the July 1 decision not to renew USMCA.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">How does it work?</strong><br>
The auto proclamation alone lists 18 pages of eligible goods; other covered items include milk, cream, wine, and hockey equipment. Duties take effect 30 days after signing, opening a negotiation window. Canada's largest export category — energy (~$130B/yr) — is carved out, as are goods already under Section 232.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Why does it matter?</strong><br>
The 30-day window sets up USMCA-adjacent talks but the auto and dairy hit lands on U.S. carmakers with cross-border supply chains and may pressure border-state consumer prices. The Canadian Chamber of Commerce called it "a regrettable escalation."
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Who is it for?</strong><br>
Trade desks, auto suppliers, USMCA-exposed importers, and anyone modeling tariff pass-through into North American consumer prices.
</p>
