<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #8a8a85; line-height: 1.5;">The AI trade breaks on payrolls Friday — Nasdaq -4.2%, SOX -8.7%, chip names lose ~$1T as Treasury yields jump and the S&amp;P 500 snaps a nine-week win streak.</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">What is it?</strong><br>
Friday June 5 delivered the worst session of 2026 for U.S. equities — Nasdaq fell 4.18% to 25,709.43, S&amp;P 500 dropped 2.64% to 7,383.74, and the Philadelphia Semiconductor Index (SOX) plunged 8.71%.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">How does it work?</strong><br>
Selling started in chips and spread: Nvidia -6%, Intel -11.3%, AMD -6.3%, Marvell -8%. Meta added fuel with an FT-reported equity raise, and a blowout May jobs report pushed 10-year yields up ~6bp to 4.544%.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Why does it matter?</strong><br>
Three things broke at once: the S&amp;P's nine-week winning streak ended; the 'AI capex always rallies' reflex cracked; and the curve repriced for a higher-for-longer Fed ahead of Warsh's first FOMC on June 16–17.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Who is it for?</strong><br>
Index allocators, semiconductor desks, and risk-parity books — CPI on June 11 and the June 16–17 FOMC are the next catalysts.
</p>
