<h2 style="margin: 0 0 8px; font-size: 24px; font-weight: 800; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.2;">Alphabet raises $84.75B — largest tech equity offering in history — to fund AI infrastructure</h2>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #8a8a85; line-height: 1.5;">Alphabet taps the equity market for $84.75B — biggest tech follow-on in market history — to fund AI compute buildout.</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">What is it?</strong><br>
On June 1, 2026 Alphabet announced an $80B equity capital raise, upsized to $84.75B at June 2 pricing. The stated use is general corporate purposes and capex to scale AI infrastructure as demand exceeds supply.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">How does it work?</strong><br>
The raise has three legs: $30B in concurrent underwritten offerings of Class A/C shares and 6.25% mandatory convertible preferred; a $40B at-the-market program selling shares into the open market over time; and a $10B private placement to Berkshire Hathaway.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Why does it matter?</strong><br>
This is the largest equity raise ever by a mega-cap tech company and the clearest signal yet that hyperscaler AI capex is consuming free cash flow faster than it's generated. GOOGL fell ~3.5% at the June 2 open on dilution math, and the ATM program creates a steady supply overhang.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Who is it for?</strong><br>
Mega-cap tech investors and anyone modeling hyperscaler AI capex or watching dilution read-throughs to MSFT, AMZN, and META (combined ~$800B in 2026 industry capex estimates).
</p>
